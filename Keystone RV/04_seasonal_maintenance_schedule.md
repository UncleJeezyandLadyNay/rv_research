# Keystone RV — Seasonal Maintenance Schedule
*Cougar Western Elevation 28BHWE · Cougar Half-Ton 28BHS · Cougar Half-Ton 29RLP · Outback 252RD*
*Date: 2026-05-23*

---

## What Makes Keystone Maintenance Specific

Keystone's construction choices affect the maintenance schedule in specific ways:

- **One-piece TPO roof** — industry standard; annual sealant inspection and re-application is non-negotiable. Keystone's documented water damage warranty exclusion ("inadequate seal maintenance") means skipping sealant maintenance can void your water damage coverage.
- **HyperCore composite walls** — more moisture-resistant than OSB but the exterior seam sealants still need annual inspection. Any breach in a wall seam allows moisture into the laminated panel from the edge.
- **HyperDeck inorganic flooring** — doesn't absorb moisture the way OSB does, but a sustained water intrusion can still damage the floor mounting structure.
- **Tru-fit slide system** — requires seal conditioning (303 Aerospace) and track lubrication same as any slide system.
- **SolarFlex (252RD)** — the MPPT controller and panel mount sealants need annual attention. Solar panels are roof penetrations.
- **BAL Norco 5.1 (252RD)** — the 5-point mount hardware needs annual inspection and torque check per BAL specs.
- **Western Elevation ABS suspension (28BHWE)** — coil-assist suspension; inspect mount hardware and rubber isolators annually, more frequently with off-road use.
- **80-gallon fresh water system (28BHWE)** — larger tank takes more antifreeze to winterize fully; confirm full tank drain before blow-out.

## Before Every Trip (15 minutes)

- [ ] Walk all four exterior sides — any new seal gaps, delamination bubbles, or corner cap damage since last trip?
- [ ] Check all compartment door latches
- [ ] Confirm propane tanks — full and regulator hose undamaged
- [ ] Run all slides out and back in once — smooth operation before leaving
- [ ] Lights check: running, brake, turn signals with tow vehicle connected
- [ ] Verify breakaway cable connected
- [ ] Tire pressure cold — verify against spec for your loaded weight
- [ ] **28BHWE:** exterior shower valve closed/capped before travel
- [ ] **252RD:** SolarFlex controller — no fault codes before departure

---

## After Every Trip (15 minutes)

- [ ] Walk exterior — note any new seal concerns or road debris impact
- [ ] Clean debris from slide rubber wiper seals before it sits
- [ ] Log any issues while fresh — rattles, leaks, anything that didn't work right
- [ ] If unit sat in rain: check interior for wet spots at ceiling, windows, or slide floor
- [ ] **28BHWE:** after any off-road use, walk the ABS suspension — any loose hardware or unusual wear at mounting points?

## Spring / Season Open (3–4 hours)
*Most important maintenance event of the year*

### Roof — Annual Inspection and Re-Sealing

Keystone's water damage exclusion depends on "inadequate seal maintenance." This is the most important maintenance task — do it every spring and document it.

- [ ] **Get on the roof** — walkable TPO; 6-foot ladder
- [ ] Walk the full roof: inspect every penetration — A/C base gaskets, vent collars, MaxxAir fans, antenna mount, any solar brackets (252RD)
- [ ] **Front and rear wall-to-roof seams** — the documented highest-risk locations for voids; press every inch
- [ ] Press every sealant bead: pliable = intact; cracking = re-seal now with Dicor 501LSW
- [ ] Inspect both A/C covers: any new cracking, missing bolts, or broken clips since last season
- [ ] **252RD:** inspect all four corners of each solar panel frame; check all panel mount point sealants — any lifting = re-seal with Dicor 501LSW
- [ ] **Photograph every sealant joint, date-stamped — warranty protection**

### Sidewalls and Exterior Seams

- [ ] Walk all four sides, press every vertical seam, corner cap, and window frame
- [ ] Re-seal any gaps or cracks with Dicor 505LSW non-sag
- [ ] Front cap seam: press along the full seam where fiberglass meets the sidewall
- [ ] **28BHWE exterior shower:** inspect the plumbing access panel seal at the wall entry

### Slide Seals

- [ ] Extend all slides
- [ ] Inspect rubber wiper seals: no tears, no gaps, no UV cracking
- [ ] Apply 303 Aerospace Protectant to all rubber slide seals
- [ ] Inspect slide membranes (top seals) — any lifting or tearing?
- [ ] Clean slide tracks with dry cloth; apply Camco Slide-Out Dry Lube (silicone-based)
- [ ] Retract slides, verify smooth operation

### De-Winterization

- [ ] Flush antifreeze from all lines — open every faucet until clear (pink method) or air-free (blow-out method)
- [ ] Install new water filter cartridge
- [ ] Pressure walk under city water for 30 minutes
- [ ] **Water heater bypass: return to normal position before running hot water**
- [ ] Flush water heater tank before use
- [ ] Test water pump on fresh tank
- [ ] **28BHWE 80-gal tank:** confirm tank fully de-winterized — larger tank requires more antifreeze or more thorough blow-out; run water through until no antifreeze taste/color

### Western Elevation ABS Suspension (28BHWE)

- [ ] Inspect all ABS suspension mount hardware — all bolts present and snug
- [ ] Check rubber isolator pads within the suspension for cracking or deterioration
- [ ] Listen for any clunking or squeaking during the first 50 miles of the season — document and address
- [ ] Torque shackle and mount bolts to spec if any movement is detected

### SolarFlex System (252RD)

- [ ] Verify MPPT controller shows normal operation — no error codes
- [ ] On a sunny day: confirm positive PV input and charging current on the display
- [ ] Inspect solar panel surfaces: any cracks, delamination, or physical damage from winter storage?
- [ ] Clean panel surfaces with soft cloth and mild soapy water — remove bird droppings and winter debris
- [ ] Verify all panel wiring connections at the combiner box are secure

### Appliances — Annual Start-Up

- [ ] Both A/C units: run 20 minutes each, cold air at all vents — clean evaporator filter
- [ ] Furnace: full heat cycle — **check cooktop and furnace orifices for spider nests**
- [ ] Light all cooktop burners, verify clean blue flame
- [ ] Water heater: both modes, verified hot
- [ ] Refrigerator: 24 hours on electric, verify below 40°F

### Chassis and Tires

- [ ] Tire sidewall inspection: cracking, date codes (replace over 5–6 years)
- [ ] Cold tire pressure per spec — all models
- [ ] **28BHWE off-road tires:** check for any sidewall damage from previous season's off-road use
- [ ] Lug nut torque — verify per spec (typically 90–100 ft-lbs; confirm for your axle)
- [ ] Wheel bearing inspection: spin each wheel, feel for roughness
- [ ] Brake inspection through wheel opening: pad wear, magnet condition

---

## Mid-Summer Check (1–2 hours)

- [ ] Roof spot-check — focus on any seals borderline at spring open; UV summer heat accelerates sealant degradation
- [ ] Re-condition slide rubber seals — second 303 Aerospace application
- [ ] **252RD solar panels:** mid-season clean and system check
- [ ] After any heavy rain: walk the interior for any wet spots at slide seals, windows, or the front wall seam
- [ ] **28BHWE:** after any off-road use, re-inspect ABS suspension hardware

---

## Fall / Season Close (3–4 hours)

### Final Seal Inspection Before Winter

- [ ] Full roof inspection — photograph and log same as spring
- [ ] Re-seal any compromised joints before winter — water under ice is worst-case
- [ ] Walk all four sidewalls — any new separation?
- [ ] **Log fall seal work: date, locations, product names, photos**

### Trailer Winterization

**Option 1 — Air Blow-Out:**
- [ ] Compressor at max 50 PSI at city water inlet via blow-out plug
- [ ] Open faucets from low points up; hold each open until dry air flows
- [ ] Drain all three tanks; leave low-point drain valves open during storage
- [ ] **28BHWE:** the 80-gallon tank holds significantly more water than a standard tank — ensure full drain before blow-out. Tilt the trailer slightly nose-down if possible to assist draining.

**Option 2 — Non-Toxic Antifreeze:**
- [ ] Drain all tanks; bypass water heater
- [ ] Run pink antifreeze through until pink appears at every faucet
- [ ] Pour antifreeze into toilet, all drains, outdoor shower line (28BHWE)
- [ ] Remove and store water filter inside

### SolarFlex Winterization (252RD)

- [ ] Verify battery bank is fully charged before winter storage
- [ ] Connect smart battery maintainer — the solar panels will continue passive charging through winter (with any sunlight), but a maintainer ensures consistent charge
- [ ] Clean solar panels before winter — accumulated debris reduces any passive solar charging

### Battery and Electrical

- [ ] Connect smart battery maintainer (not trickle) during storage
- [ ] Disconnect battery if storing in extreme cold — bring inside if possible
- [ ] Cover A/C shrouds — prevents debris and animal nesting

### Exterior and Tires

- [ ] Apply 303 Aerospace to all rubber moldings and exterior seals before storage
- [ ] Cover tires if storing outdoors — UV tire covers
- [ ] Move trailer monthly during storage to prevent flat spots
- [ ] **28BHWE:** cap the exterior LP quick-connect and seal the exterior shower supply lines if not already done for blow-out

## Annual Service Items (Every 12 Months)

- [ ] Wheel bearing inspection and repack (packed bearings) or inspection (sealed)
- [ ] Brake inspection — pad wear, magnet condition
- [ ] Lug nut torque — re-torque after first 50 miles of season and at 12 months
- [ ] Smoke detector battery replacement
- [ ] CO and propane detector test — replace if past rated service life (5–7 years)
- [ ] Fire extinguisher charge check
- [ ] A/C coil cleaning — spray coil cleaner, not pressure water
- [ ] Water heater anode rod inspection (LP/electric combo heaters)
- [ ] **252RD BAL Norco 5.1:** all mount point hardware torque check per BAL specification
- [ ] **28BHWE ABS suspension:** all mount hardware and rubber isolators
- [ ] **252RD SolarFlex:** MPPT controller firmware update check (if applicable)

## The Maintenance Log

One entry per event:
1. Date
2. What you inspected
3. What you found (including "nothing to report")
4. What you did (product name, specific location)
5. Photos attached

**Critical for Keystone specifically:** Keystone's documented warranty claim resistance means a thorough maintenance log is your best weapon if a warranty claim is disputed. "Inadequate seal maintenance" is the most common reason Keystone denies water damage claims — a dated log with roof photos from each season makes that argument untenable. Keep it current and keep the photos.
