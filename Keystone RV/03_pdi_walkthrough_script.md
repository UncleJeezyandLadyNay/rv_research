# Keystone RV — PDI Walkthrough Script
*Word-for-word script for use at the dealership*
*Cougar Western Elevation 28BHWE · Cougar Half-Ton 28BHS · Cougar Half-Ton 29RLP · Outback 252RD*
*Date: 2026-05-23*

**Time budget: 3–4 hours.** Keystone has the largest dealer network in the industry — which means tech experience varies enormously by dealer. Some Keystone dealers have delivered hundreds of Cougar and Outback units and know every failure point. Others push volume and rush PDIs. Ask when you book the appointment: how many Keystone trailers does this service department deliver per month?

---

## The Day Before — Call the Dealer

> *"I'm coming in tomorrow for my PDI on the [model]. Before I arrive, I need the unit on shore power, city water connected, and the water heater switched on in electric mode at least 30 minutes before I get there. I also need the refrigerator running for at least four hours. Plan for three to four hours for the walkthrough."*

If they hesitate on the water heater:

> *"I've read about a documented factory issue where the 120V water heater connection isn't made at the factory. Turning it on 30 minutes early lets me verify it works before we start. It takes 30 minutes — I'd rather know immediately than discover it after I've driven home."*

---

## Arrival — Set the Frame (5 minutes)

> *"I've done specific research on [the Cougar/Outback] and I have a checklist. I want to go through everything systematically — three to four hours. I know Keystone builds a popular trailer and I'm excited about this one; I just want to make sure my specific unit came through correctly. Fair?"*

Then:

> *"Can I have a copy of the PDI form your team completed? And I'll need a signed copy at the end with anything we find today added."*

---

## Water Heater — START HERE (5 minutes, already running)

> *"I asked you to start the water heater 30 minutes ago — let's verify it first. I've read about a documented factory defect on Keystone units where the 120V connection to the water heater element isn't made at the factory. I want hot water before I do anything else."*

Turn on the hot tap at the kitchen sink and wait:

> *"I need it actually hot — not warm, not lukewarm. Hot."*

If the water heater produces cold or tepid water after 30+ minutes on electric:

> *"The heater isn't working on electric mode. Let's switch to propane and test that too."*

If either mode fails:

> *"I want this repaired and both modes retested before we continue. This is a safety issue — a water heater failure can cascade into worse problems. Can the service department look at the wiring now?"*

---

## Tire Pressure — Before Anything Moves (5 minutes)

> *"Before this trailer moves at all, I want to check tire pressure. I've found documentation of all four tires being under-inflated by 15 PSI at delivery on Keystone units. Under-inflated tires at this weight create heat and blowout risk."*

Check every tire with your own gauge:

> *"What's the spec for these tires? I want to see the door placard and I'll cross-reference with the tire sidewall."*

If tires are under spec:

> *"These need to be inflated to spec before I do anything else. Can your service team do that now?"*

Don't let the dealer brush this off. It takes ten minutes with an air compressor. If they won't do it, you should be concerned about the rest of the PDI.

---

## Shower Pan — First 5 Minutes Inside (5 minutes)

> *"I want to test the shower pan support before we do the full interior walk. This is a documented issue on Keystone trailers — the pan is installed without adequate sub-support and flexes underfoot."*

Step into the shower pan with full weight. Bounce:

> *"Do you feel that flex? That's the pan without support underneath. It will crack over time and water will leak into the subfloor."*

If the pan flexes at all:

> *"I'd like the service department to add under-pan support before I take delivery. I know this is a known issue — your techs should know the fix. Can we get that scheduled?"*

A rigid pan that does not flex at all means the support is adequate. Document on the PDI form regardless.

---

## Drain Plumbing — Verify Before Filling Black Tank (10 minutes)

> *"I want to confirm the drain plumbing is correct before we continue. There's a documented factory defect where drain lines get crossed at installation. I want to verify gray goes to gray and black goes to black."*

Run the kitchen and bathroom sinks for several minutes to add some water to the gray tank:

> *"Now pull the gray valve — I want to see it drain. Don't touch the black valve."*

Watch the gray drain. Then:

> *"Now tell me which handle is labeled black. I'm going to note both on the PDI form."*

If any behavior is inconsistent with the labeling:

> *"Something doesn't match. I want a service tech to trace the plumbing before I sign — crossed drains mean black tank waste can come out of the wrong valve. That's a health and odor issue I'm not willing to inherit."*

---

## Roof Walk — Full Inspection (15 minutes)

> *"I want to walk the roof. Can we get a ladder?"*

On the roof, start with the A/C covers:

> *"I've read about cracked A/C covers with missing bolts at delivery. Let me check both covers."*

Press on each A/C cover — it should be rigid, no cracks, all mounting bolts present.

Then walk every sealant joint:

> *"I'm going to press every sealant bead on this roof. Front and rear wall-to-roof seams specifically — those are the documented void locations."*

Press each bead with your fingernail. Any cracking or separation:

> *"This sealant has failed. I need it re-applied with Dicor 501LSW before I take delivery. This is the leading cause of water damage under Keystone's warranty exclusion — if I leave with failed sealant, I lose warranty coverage on any resulting water damage."*

While on the roof:

> *"I'm photographing every sealant joint with a timestamp. This is my warranty documentation baseline."*

**252RD specific:** Check all solar panel mount points:

> *"Every solar panel bracket needs to be sealed and the panel frames pressed at the corners — these are roof penetrations."*

---

## Water System Walk (15 minutes)

> *"Is city water connected? Let's bring pressure up slowly and walk the full system."*

> *"I'll be checking under every sink cabinet while the water's on. Can you keep an eye on the floor for any drips while I'm under the cabinets?"*

After the full pressurized walk:

> *"Let's run the shower for 5 minutes — I want to see if the pan stays dry around the door seal and under the pan lip."*

---

## Slides — Full Extension and Retraction

> *"Let's extend all slides."*

Watch each slide extend fully:

> *"I'm walking the seal perimeter on every slide — all four sides of the wiper seal on every slide that extends."*

**29RLP with two slides:**

> *"I want to extend both slides simultaneously and verify the controller handles both without errors or hesitation."*

Retract all slides — smooth operation, no grinding.

---

## Outback 252RD — SolarFlex and Stabilization

> *"I want to run the SolarFlex system. Can you point me to the MPPT controller?"*

On a sunny day:

> *"The controller should show positive PV input and charging current. Can you confirm the system is actively charging?"*

Then deploy the BAL Norco 5.1 stabilization:

> *"Let me deploy the full five-point system. I want to compare how much the trailer rocks now versus standard scissor jacks — this is one of the Outback's key selling features."*

Rock the trailer side to side with all five points deployed:

> *"Good. I want to inspect all five mount points — all hardware present and snug?"*

Check the U-dinette seating:

> *"I'm going to push hard on all the seat backs and then lift the seat cushions to look at the base support structure. I've read about Keystone Outback dinette seats coming apart because they're held with staples instead of screws."*

If you find staple-only construction:

> *"These seats are stapled together. I'd like the service department to add proper screw fasteners to the seat support structures before I take delivery. Staple-only construction fails at the connections within a season."*

---

## Appliances (15 minutes)

> *"Let's work through all the appliances."*

- Both A/C units: 5 minutes each, cold air at every vent
- Furnace: full heat cycle, warm at all registers
- Cooktop: all burners, clean ignition
- Microwave: 60 seconds on water
- Water heater: already verified at start — confirm still hot

---

## Final Questions Before Signing

> *"What's your typical turnaround time for Keystone warranty parts? I know Keystone's warranty process can be slow — I want to understand what to expect if I file a claim in year one."*

> *"If I have a warranty issue Keystone is slow to approve, what can you do on your end to escalate it? Do you have a Keystone rep you work with directly?"*

> *"If any warranty repair takes more than 30 days, what's your loaner or reimbursement policy?"*

> *"Do you have a service tech who specifically knows the [Cougar Western Elevation / Outback] line, or does the same shop handle all brands?"*

The answers tell you whether this dealer will be a partner in warranty work or just a bottleneck.

---

## Walkthrough Wrap-Up

> *"Here's my complete list."*

State everything found. Then:

> *"Everything fixable today, I'd like fixed and retested before I sign. For anything that needs scheduling — shower pan support, dinette fasteners, sealant repairs — I want a specific date in writing. I'll come back when it's ready."*

If you hear "we'll handle it after you take delivery":

> *"Before I sign, these are your cost to fix — no approval process, no waiting for warranty authorization. Once I drive away, each of these becomes a warranty claim. I'm patient enough to come back when the work is done. Can we set a date?"*

- [ ] All slides extended and retracted cleanly
- [ ] SolarFlex controller active and charging (252RD)
