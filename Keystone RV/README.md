Keystone is volume production at its most accessible. The brand's advantage is price, half-ton towability, and the largest dealer network in the industry — if you have a warranty issue, there's almost certainly an authorized Keystone dealer within a reasonable drive. The trade-off is that factory workmanship is inconsistent, and Keystone's reputation for warranty claim resistance is well-documented on keystoneforums.com.

> **The honest positioning:** Keystone is the default choice for a large segment of the market because it's everywhere and competitively priced. The Western Elevation's off-road package and the Outback's standard SolarFlex are genuine differentiators. But buyer beware — the PDI on any Keystone unit is non-negotiable, and warranty disputes are common enough that you should enter ownership knowing how to escalate. The 3-year transferable structural warranty is a real asset; whether Keystone honors it without a fight is the variable.

## Lines Covered

### Cougar Sub-Lines
Both the **Western Elevation** and **Half-Ton** floor plans covered here are part of the Cougar family — Keystone's flagship travel trailer line. They share the same HyperCore construction and half-ton rating but differ in focus:

- **Western Elevation (28BHWE)** — Off-road/western-lifestyle package: ABS suspension, 80-gal fresh water, off-road tires, Climate Guard thermal protection, exterior connections. This is the overlander-adjacent Cougar.
- **Half-Ton (28BHS / 29RLP)** — Standard Cougar Half-Ton without the off-road package. Focuses on campground livability and half-ton tow weight.

### Outback
The **Outback** is a separate Keystone line focused on outdoor-lifestyle camping with SolarFlex solar standard, the BAL Norco 5.1 stabilization system, and a comfort-oriented floor plan design. More campground-focused than the Western Elevation.

## ⚠️ 5 Documented Factory Defects — Verify at PDI

Documented across [keystoneforums.com](https://www.keystoneforums.com/), [iRV2](https://www.irv2.com/), and [RV Reports](https://rvreports.ai/keystone-rv-complaints-a-shoppers-guide-to-leaks-frame-flex-slide-issues-and-dealer-delays/):

| # | Defect | Test |
|---|---|---|
| 🔴 1 | **Water heater not wired at factory** — 120V connection never made; unit produces only cold water from day one | Turn on water heater electric mode; verify hot water at kitchen and bath after full cycle (30 min) |
| 🔴 2 | **Tires under-inflated by 15+ PSI at delivery** — documented at PDI inspections across multiple forum reports | Check tire pressure cold with a gauge before moving the trailer; inflate to spec before any road use |
| 🔴 3 | **Shower pan unsupported / flexing** — pan installed without adequate sub-support; eventual cracking | Step into shower pan and bounce gently; any flex means support is missing — require service dept. reinforcement before signing |
| 🔴 4 | **Crossed drain plumbing** — documented factory error where drain lines are crossed at installation | Run water in kitchen and bathroom simultaneously, observe which drain gurgles; ask dealer to confirm drain routing |
| 🔴 5 | **Roof sealant voids + cracked A/C cover** — documented at PDI inspections; cracked A/C shroud cover and voids in sealant at roof seams | Get on the roof; press every sealant bead; inspect A/C covers for cracks and verify all mounting bolts are present |

## Warranty — 1-Year Base + 3-Year Structural, Both Transferable

| Coverage | Duration |
|---|---|
| Full unit (workmanship + components) | 1 year |
| Structural (frame, walls, roof, floors, slide boxes) | **3 years** |
| SolarFlex system (Outback) | Covered under structural warranty |
| Transferable to subsequent buyers | **Yes — both warranties fully transferable** |

**What to know:**
- The transferable 3-year structural warranty is legitimately competitive — same coverage period as Grand Design's structural warranty
- **Keystone warranty claim resistance is well-documented.** Multiple keystoneforums.com threads describe months-long battles over warranted repairs. Document everything, photograph everything, and communicate in writing.
- Water damage from "inadequate maintenance" is excluded — same language as all brands. Photograph all exterior seals at PDI.
- Component warranties (fridge, A/C, etc.) go to respective manufacturers (Dometic, Lippert, etc.)
