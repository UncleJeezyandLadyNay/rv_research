# Keystone RV — PDI Checklist
*Cougar Western Elevation 28BHWE · Cougar Half-Ton 28BHS · Cougar Half-Ton 29RLP · Outback 252RD*
*Date: 2026-05-23*

Print this. Bring it to the dealership. Do not sign until every item is checked.

**Time budget: 3–4 hours.** Keystone's dealer network is the largest in the industry — but "authorized dealer" doesn't mean "experienced tech." Ask when booking: "Has the tech doing my PDI delivered this model before?" A seasoned Keystone tech will know the known failure points. An inexperienced tech will rush through.

**The PDI is especially important on Keystone.** Warranty claim resistance is documented. Issues caught at PDI are the dealer's cost to fix before you sign. Issues discovered after you drive home become warranty claims — which may take months to resolve and may face resistance.

---

## 🔴 CRITICAL — Factory-Documented Defects (Start Here)

### 1. Water Heater Wiring — All Models
**Known defect:** The 120V connection to the electric water heater element is not made at the factory on documented Keystone units. The water heater won't heat on electric mode — and in some documented cases, the propane mode was also improperly connected.

- [ ] Turn on the water heater (electric mode) 30 minutes before you start the rest of the PDI
- [ ] After 30 minutes: run the hot water tap at the kitchen sink
- [ ] **Hot water must be hot** — not warm, not tepid. If it's cold or barely warm, the heater is not wired
- [ ] Switch to propane mode; verify it lights and holds
- [ ] If either mode doesn't work: **do not accept the unit until the water heater is repaired and both modes are verified hot**

---

### 2. Tire Pressure — All Models
**Known defect:** Documented PDI inspections found all four tires under-inflated by 15+ PSI at delivery. Driving on under-inflated tires at loaded weight creates heat, accelerates wear, and can cause blowouts.

- [ ] Check every tire's cold pressure with your own gauge **before the unit moves for any reason**
- [ ] Inflate to the spec on the tire sidewall or door placard (whichever is lower for the load rating)
- [ ] **28BHWE:** off-road rated tires may have different spec than the door placard — verify the correct PSI for your tire model
- [ ] Check the spare if equipped (28BHWE typically has a spare) — its pressure deteriorates over time sitting unused

---

### 3. Shower Pan Support — All Models
**Known defect:** The shower pan is installed without adequate sub-support on documented Keystone units. The pan flexes underfoot and eventually cracks, causing water to escape under the trailer floor — a source of structural damage.

- [ ] Step into the shower pan with full body weight
- [ ] Bounce slightly — any flex, movement, or "give" underfoot means the support is inadequate
- [ ] Press on the pan perimeter with your foot — it should be absolutely rigid
- [ ] If any flex is felt: require the service department to add under-pan support before you sign. This is a known issue — a seasoned tech will know the fix.

---

### 4. Crossed Drain Plumbing — All Models
**Known defect:** Drain lines have been installed crossed at the factory — the kitchen drain routes to the black tank, or other misconnections that can cause odor problems and waste water management failures.

- [ ] Have the dealer fill the gray tank slightly by running the kitchen and bathroom sinks for several minutes
- [ ] Pull the gray tank dump valve — gray tank (kitchen/bath sink water) should drain
- [ ] Verify the black tank valve is a separate handle — confirm it doesn't drain when you pull gray
- [ ] If the labeling or behavior seems wrong: insist the dealer verify drain routing before you sign

---

### 5. Roof Sealant and A/C Cover — All Models
**Known defect:** PDI inspections have found cracked A/C cover shrouds with missing mounting bolts, and voids or cracking in the lap sealant at front and rear roof seams.

- [ ] Get on the roof — Keystone trailers have walkable TPO roofs; use a 6-foot ladder
- [ ] Inspect both A/C unit covers: any cracks, broken clips, or missing mounting bolts?
- [ ] Walk the full roof and press every sealant bead at every penetration: A/C bases, vent collars, antenna, any solar panel brackets (252RD)
- [ ] **Press the front and rear wall-to-roof seams specifically** — these are the documented void locations
- [ ] Any void or cracking = re-apply Dicor 501LSW before leaving the lot
- [ ] **Photograph every sealant joint and the A/C covers — date-stamped**

## Western Elevation 28BHWE — Specific Checks

- [ ] **80-gallon fresh water fill:** fill the tank to verify the fill inlet, plumbing, and tank gauge work correctly — don't accept "we'll test it" without water in it
- [ ] **ABS suspension:** after loading the trailer for the PDI drive-out, listen for any squeaks or clunks from the suspension; document any abnormal noise before leaving
- [ ] **Exterior hot water shower:** run the exterior hot/cold shower — hot must be hot, cold must be cold; check the supply line connections under the exterior shower panel for any drips
- [ ] **Exterior LP quick-connect:** verify the connection point is capped, sealed, and the fitting is the correct standard LP quick-connect type for your accessories
- [ ] **Off-road tires:** inspect for any transport damage to sidewalls; verify correct PSI (off-road tires can have different ratings than standard trailer tires)
- [ ] **Dual propane tanks:** verify both are full and both regulators switch correctly
- [ ] **Climate Guard:** no specific test beyond sealing inspections — the Climate Guard rating comes from the insulation package, which you can't verify visually. Verify the underbelly is fully enclosed and no gaps exist in the belly wrap.

---

## Outback 252RD — Specific Checks

- [ ] **SolarFlex 220 system:** on a sunny day, verify the solar controller shows PV input and charging current — the system should be actively charging the battery bank
- [ ] If cloudy: verify the MPPT controller is present, wired, and shows the correct battery voltage (no error codes)
- [ ] **BAL Norco 5.1 stabilization:** deploy all five stabilizer points, rock the trailer from side to side and front to back — compare to a standard 4-point system (dramatically less movement expected)
- [ ] Inspect the BAL Norco mounting hardware: all bolts present and snug
- [ ] **U-dinette seating:** push hard on all dinette seat backs and pads; lift seat cushions and check the base support structure — documented use of staples instead of proper screws on some Outback units
- [ ] **On Demand water heater (if equipped):** flow trigger must activate the unit; verify hot water at both kitchen and bath

## Exterior Walk

### Roof (15 minutes)

- [ ] Walk the full roof — all four corners, center, every penetration
- [ ] Inspect all sealant joints per Critical section above
- [ ] Check the rear cap seam (where TPO roof meets the rear fiberglass) — common void location
- [ ] Look at the roof rack area near A/C units for any cracked mounting fins
- [ ] **252RD solar panels:** all panel mounts sealed; no lifting at corners of panel frames
- [ ] **Photograph every sealant joint, date-stamped**

### Sidewalls and Seams

- [ ] Walk all four sides, press every vertical seam, corner cap, and window trim
- [ ] Any soft spots in the wall panel = moisture already present — stop and investigate
- [ ] Inspect all compartment doors: open, close, and latch flush
- [ ] **28BHWE:** check the exterior shower plumbing access panel — properly sealed at the wall entry

### Awning

- [ ] Deploy the full awning, let it extend fully, walk the arm and rail for misalignment
- [ ] Retract smoothly — no grinding or hesitation

---

## Slide-Out Walk — All Models

- [ ] Extend all slides fully
- [ ] Inspect rubber wiper seals on all four sides of every slide — no tears or gaps
- [ ] Walk on the slide floor — should feel solid, no flex different from the main floor
- [ ] Inspect slide membrane (top seal) — any lifting or tearing?
- [ ] Retract slides — smooth Tru-fit operation, no grinding
- [ ] **29RLP:** two slides — test both independently, then simultaneously; verify the controller handles both without error

---

## Water System

- [ ] Shore water connected before starting this section
- [ ] Connect city water slowly — walk full trailer under pressure for 15 minutes
- [ ] Open every faucet: kitchen, bathroom, shower, exterior shower (28BHWE)
- [ ] Look under every sink cabinet for drips
- [ ] Flush toilets — clean bowl clear, no running after flush
- [ ] Verify dump valve labels match actual tanks (per Critical section above)
- [ ] Test water pump on fresh tank; prime and pressurize

---

## Electrical

- [ ] Test every 120V outlet with a plug tester
- [ ] Test every 12V outlet and USB charging point
- [ ] Trip and reset the GFCI master outlet; verify correct downstream outlets respond
- [ ] **252RD:** verify SolarFlex controller display — correct voltage, charging current visible
- [ ] Verify inverter/charger display — no fault codes
- [ ] Check battery bank charge state — all batteries equal

---

## Appliances

- [ ] **Water heater:** both modes hot as specified in Critical section above
- [ ] **Refrigerator:** running 4+ hours; verify below 40°F with thermometer
- [ ] **Cooktop:** all burners light cleanly, hold flame
- [ ] **Microwave:** heat water 60 seconds
- [ ] **Furnace:** run full heat cycle, warm air at all registers
- [ ] **Both A/C units:** run 5 minutes each, feel for cold air at every vent
- [ ] **Range hood:** all speeds, correct exhaust direction

---

## Interior

- [ ] Push on every wall-mounted component: TV brackets, shelving, towel bars
- [ ] Open every cabinet and drawer — smooth slides, closes flush
- [ ] **Dinette seating (especially 252RD):** push hard on seat backs, lift cushions, inspect support structure
- [ ] Walk the full floor — no soft spots, no squeaking
- [ ] Inspect bunk ladders (28BHWE/28BHS): ladder hardware solid, no loose bolts
- [ ] Push laterally on the bunk structure — rigid, no movement
- [ ] Entry door: latches, dead bolt, screen door

---

## Chassis and Underside

- [ ] Walk underside with flashlight: underbelly intact, no sections hanging loose or separated
- [ ] **Specifically check the front underbelly section** — complete underbelly separation on the road is documented on Keystone units; press the front underbelly wrap
- [ ] All drain lines secured, no kinks
- [ ] Spare tire bracket secure (28BHWE with spare)
- [ ] All running lights and brake lights working with tow vehicle connected

## Before You Sign — Non-Negotiables

- [ ] **Signed PDI form** with all findings documented and both parties' signatures
- [ ] **Water heater verified hot** — both electric and propane modes
- [ ] **Tires at correct PSI** — verified with your gauge before any movement
- [ ] **Shower pan rigid** — no flex under full body weight
- [ ] **Drain plumbing confirmed correct** — gray drains gray, black drains black
- [ ] **Roof sealant pressed** — no voids, A/C covers intact with all bolts
- [ ] **252RD U-dinette structure solid** — no staples-only construction visible
- [ ] **All "fix before delivery" items resolved** or scheduled in writing with specific date
- [ ] **Dated photos of every exterior seal** on all four sides and roof
- [ ] **Full warranty documentation** in hand — 1-year base, 3-year structural
- [ ] **Service department direct contact** — name, phone, email
- [ ] **Registered with Keystone on delivery day** at keystonerv.com

## Print-and-Bring Summary — 5 Critical Tests

1. **Water heater:** turn on 30 min before PDI starts; both modes must produce hot water
2. **Tire pressure:** check every tire cold with your gauge before the unit moves
3. **Shower pan:** full body weight, bounce — zero flex means it's supported
4. **Drain routing:** confirm gray-to-gray, black-to-black
5. **Roof:** get up there, press every sealant bead, inspect both A/C covers for cracks and missing bolts

**If any of these five fail: do not sign. Pre-signature = dealer's cost. Post-signature = warranty claim.**
