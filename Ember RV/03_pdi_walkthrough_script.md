# Ember RV — PDI Walkthrough Script
*Word-for-word script for use at the dealership*
*Overland 221MSL · E-Series 26ETS · Touring Edition 2500RDL*
*Date: 2026-05-23*

**Time budget: 3–4 hours.** Ember's dealer network is smaller than Alliance or Grand Design — your dealer may have delivered a handful of Ember units or dozens, depending on their volume. Ask when you book the PDI: "How many Ember units does your service department deliver per year?" The answer calibrates how much guidance you'll need to provide vs. receive.

---

## The Day Before — Call the Dealer

> *"I'm coming in tomorrow for my PDI on the Ember [floor plan]. Before I get there, can you make sure the unit is connected to shore power, city water is hooked up, and the refrigerator has been running at least four hours? I'll also need the EmberLink app paired and working before I arrive. Plan for three to four hours."*

If your floor plan is the 2500RDL with auto-leveling:

> *"I'd also like the Ground Control TT3 auto-leveling system demonstrated on uneven ground before I arrive. Can you have the tech park it on a slope and run a full cycle so it's ready to show me?"*

---

## Arrival — Set the Frame (5 minutes)

> *"I've done a lot of research specifically on Ember — the EmberTrack system is one of the reasons I chose this brand, and I want to make sure my unit came through correctly. I have a checklist and I'll need three to four hours. Fair?"*

Immediately:

> *"Can I get a copy of the PDI form your team completed? And I'll need a signed copy at the end with anything we find today added."*

One opening question to calibrate the tech:

> *"Has your service team delivered many Ember units? I want to know if you're familiar with the Ground Control TT3 [or EmberTrack system] before we start."*

---

## GFCI Circuit — START HERE (10 minutes)

This is the fastest test to run and the most commonly documented factory error on Ember units.

> *"Before we do anything else, I want to check the GFCI wiring. I've read about a factory wiring issue where the 20-amp GFCI breaker is incorrectly wired and trips as soon as you plug anything in."*

Plug your phone charger into an outlet near the kitchen. Then ask the dealer to plug a coffee maker into a different outlet and turn it on. Both should run simultaneously.

> *"Good — both running, no trip. Now let me trip the GFCI."*

Push the test button on the GFCI outlet. Walk the trailer and identify which outlets go dead. Reset. Verify all restore.

> *"I want to see that the correct outlets — and only the correct outlets — are on that circuit. If anything is miswired, I want it on the PDI form before we go any further."*

---

## Water System — Full Pressurized Walk (20 minutes)

> *"Is city water connected? I want to bring the pressure up slowly and walk the entire water system before we continue."*

Ask the dealer tech to open the city water connection slowly — not full blast.

> *"I've read about loose PEX connections at bathroom faucet lines on Ember units — I want to be under the vanity cabinet with the water running."*

Get down and physically look at the braided PVC fittings under the bathroom vanity while the tech keeps the water running. Feel the fittings with your hand.

Then walk every other cabinet under every other sink:

> *"I'm walking every sink — kitchen, outside shower if equipped — looking for any drips under pressure."*

After the walk:

> *"Let's test the dump valves. I want to pull each one and confirm the label matches what actually drains."*

---

## Stabilizer Legs / Auto-Leveling (10–15 minutes)

**E-Series 26ETS and Overland 221MSL:**

> *"Let's deploy all the stabilizer legs. I've read about a factory wiring defect where the crimp connectors aren't properly stripped and the leg won't deploy electrically."*

Watch each leg extend fully. Then:

> *"I'm going to push down on each corner of the trailer. I want to confirm the legs are actually bearing weight, not just touching the ground."*

**Touring Edition 2500RDL:**

> *"I want to run the full Ground Control TT3 auto-leveling cycle from scratch. Can we park on uneven ground if we're not already?"*

Watch the full cycle: all five points should extend, system settles and confirms level, no error codes.

> *"Good. Now I want to connect the EmberLink app on my phone and run the manual override — I want to be able to extend and retract each jack independently from my phone before I leave."*

If the system errors on "batteries low" with a full charge:

> *"This is the same calibration issue documented on the Lippert Ground Control system across multiple brands. Can your tech run the re-zero procedure right now? I want it cycling correctly before I sign."*

---

## Slide-Out Floor — 26ETS (Critical) / All Models (Inspect)

> *"Let's extend the slide. Once it's out, I want to inspect the floor edge seam specifically — I've read about a water intrusion issue on the 26ETS where the slide floor swelled at the fiberglass edge."*

With the slide fully extended:

> *"I'm pressing along the entire perimeter of the slide floor where it meets the fiberglass. I'm looking for any bubbling, gaps, or soft spots."*

Walk across the slide floor:

> *"I'm also walking the full floor — any soft spots or inconsistency underfoot that's different from the rest of the trailer is a flag."*

---

## EmberTrack System — Hands-On (15 minutes)

This is Ember's signature feature. Test it like a buyer who will actually use it.

> *"I want to test the EmberTrack system. Can you show me how to reconfigure the rear space? I want to go from the current configuration to an open cargo bay and back."*

Watch the tech do it once, then do it yourself:

> *"I'm going to push on every anchor point after I lock each component — I want to feel that it's actually seated in the track."*

If bunks are part of the package:

> *"I'm going to climb on both bunks and apply side force — I want to know they're solid before I put a kid up there."*

Finish with:

> *"Can you show me where the weight limit markers are? I want to confirm the installation is under the 300-lb-per-position rating."*

---

## Roof Walk (15 minutes)

> *"I want to walk the roof. Can we get a ladder?"*

On the roof, start at the 221MSL's stargazer skylight (if applicable):

> *"This skylight is the largest roof penetration on this trailer — I'm going to press the full perimeter seal and photograph it."*

Walk every other sealant joint:

> *"I'm photographing every A/C base, vent collar, and the four roof corners with a timestamp. This is my warranty baseline."*

On the E-Series 26ETS's Tufflex PVC roof:

> *"The Tufflex roof should be tight and smooth with no bubbling or pulling. I'm pressing the four corners — these are the highest-failure spots."*

---

## Appliances (15 minutes)

> *"Let's run everything — A/C, furnace, cooktop, refrigerator, water heater, and the air fryer convection if equipped."*

- **12V compressor fridge (221MSL):** check temperature with thermometer — must be below 40°F. Note: the 12V compressor fridge will sound louder than a residential fridge; this is normal.
- **Air fryer convection (221MSL):** test both microwave and air fryer modes
- **All cooktop burners:** light each one, confirm clean ignition, hold flame
- **Furnace:** full heat cycle, warm air at all registers
- **A/C units:** 5 minutes each, feel for cold air at every vent
- **Water heater:** both electric and propane modes if LP equipped

---

## Final Questions Before Signing

> *"What's your average turnaround time for Ember warranty parts? I've read about a case where an inverter took over two months — I want to know what to expect."*

> *"If I have an issue and you can't get parts within a reasonable timeframe, does Ember authorize me to use another service center? Is that in the warranty?"*

> *"Is there an Ember tech on staff who specifically knows the EmberLink system and Ground Control TT3 [if buying 2500RDL]? Or will any RV tech handle warranty repairs?"*

> *"Who do I contact at Ember directly — not just through the dealer — if I have a warranty dispute?"*

These answers tell you how your ownership experience will go before you commit.

---

## Walkthrough Wrap-Up

> *"Here's my list of items for the PDI form."*

State everything found. Then:

> *"Anything fixable today, I'd like fixed and retested before I sign. For anything that needs a part or scheduling — GFCI rewiring, PEX connection replacement, slide floor edge repair — I want a specific date written on the PDI form. I'll come back."*

If the dealer pushes to "handle it under warranty after you drive home":

> *"I understand, but I'd rather not take delivery until these are resolved. Before I sign, these are your cost to fix with no warranty claim process. After I sign, each of these becomes a warranty claim that requires parts approval and scheduling. Let's get them right today."*

---

## Final Checklist Before Keys Change Hands

- [ ] Signed PDI form with all findings documented
- [ ] GFCI circuit verified — dual appliance test and correct outlet mapping
- [ ] All PEX connections dry under pressure — you verified under the bathroom vanity yourself
- [ ] All stabilizer legs deployed and confirmed under load — or Ground Control TT3 cycle complete (2500RDL)
- [ ] Slide floor edge pressed — no swelling, no gap
- [ ] Every EmberTrack component locked and tested under force
- [ ] Awning deployed and retracted twice without hesitation
- [ ] Stargazer skylight seal pressed and photographed (221MSL)
- [ ] Refrigerator below 40°F confirmed with your thermometer
- [ ] EmberLink app connected to your phone — auto-leveling controlled from app (2500RDL)
- [ ] All appliances tested and functioning
- [ ] All "fix before delivery" items resolved OR scheduled in writing with specific date
- [ ] Dated photos of every exterior seal — all four sides and roof
- [ ] Full 2-year warranty documentation in hand
- [ ] Service department direct contact: name, phone, email
- [ ] Registered with Ember RV on delivery day
