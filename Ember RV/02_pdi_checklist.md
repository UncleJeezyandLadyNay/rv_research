# Ember RV — PDI Checklist
*Overland 221MSL · E-Series 26ETS · Touring Edition 2500RDL*
*Date: 2026-05-23*

Print this. Bring it to the dealership. Do not sign until every item is checked.

**Time budget: 3–4 hours.** Ember's dealer network is smaller than Alliance or Grand Design, which means the technicians may have less experience with Ember-specific systems (EmberTrack, EmberLink, Ground Control TT3). Ask upfront if there is a technician who has delivered Ember units before. If not, slow down and test everything yourself.

---

## 🔴 CRITICAL — Factory-Documented Defects (Start Here)

### 1. GFCI Circuit Wiring — All Models
**Known defect:** Same 20A GFCI miswiring documented on Ember that is also a known defect on Alliance Delta. The breaker trips immediately when anything is plugged in — the wiring is reversed at the factory.

- [ ] Shore power connected
- [ ] Plug a phone charger or lamp into every interior 120V outlet — verify each delivers power
- [ ] Run microwave + coffee maker (or any two appliances) simultaneously — no trip
- [ ] Trip the GFCI master outlet; verify correct downstream outlets go dead
- [ ] Reset and confirm all outlets restore

---

### 2. PEX Water Connection Leaks — All Models (especially 26ETS)
**Known defect:** Braided PVC connections at bathroom faucet hot and cold lines are documented to loosen in transit. Also found on refrigerator water line connections if equipped.

- [ ] Shore water connected before starting this test
- [ ] Connect city water **slowly** — bring pressure up gradually, not full blast
- [ ] Walk every cabinet under every sink while water is pressurized
- [ ] **Get your head under the bathroom vanity cabinet specifically** — look and feel for moisture on the PEX fittings
- [ ] Run hot and cold at every faucet for 2 minutes each
- [ ] Look for slow drips at every connection point — drips that start slow get worse on the road

---

### 3. Stabilizer Leg Wiring — All Models (especially 221MSL)
**Known defect:** Crimp connectors at stabilizer leg motors not fully stripped at factory — the leg won't deploy electrically or deploys inconsistently.

- [ ] Deploy all stabilizer legs electrically — each leg must extend fully
- [ ] Push down on each corner of the trailer — legs should be solid, no flex or bounce
- [ ] Retract all legs — smooth, complete retraction
- [ ] If any leg won't deploy or only partially deploys: **do not accept until wiring is repaired and retested**
- [ ] **2500RDL specific:** the Ground Control TT3 auto-leveling system handles stabilization automatically — run a full auto-level cycle on uneven ground and verify all five points lock

---

### 4. Slide-Out Floor Integrity — 26ETS (documented); all models (inspect)
**Known defect:** Slide-out floor painted plywood swelled on documented 26ETS units, with water tracking around the fiberglass edge seam during rain.

- [ ] Extend the slide fully
- [ ] Get down and look at the slide floor surface — any bubbling, paint lifting, or soft spots?
- [ ] Press along the full perimeter where the slide floor meets the fiberglass edge — any gap or lifting seam?
- [ ] Walk across the slide floor — no soft spots or flex inconsistent with the surrounding floor
- [ ] Check the floor-to-wall seam all the way around the slide opening — no separation, no daylight

---

### 5. EmberTrack Anchor Points and Installed Components — All Models
**Known defect:** EmberTrack E-Track brackets, bunk components, and accessory anchors have been found improperly seated or misaligned at factory installation. Also: shower control fixtures, cabinet doors, and other components installed upside-down or misaligned.

- [ ] Push hard on every EmberTrack component currently mounted — slide/rotate each one to confirm it's locked into the track
- [ ] If bunks are configured: climb on each bunk and rock it — hold on to the frame and apply side force
- [ ] Open and close every cabinet door — hinges should be straight, door should close flush without gap
- [ ] Test shower control fixture: cold is cold, hot is hot, correct rotation direction
- [ ] Check range hood fan direction — air should exhaust outward, not inward

---

## Exterior Walk

### Roof (15 minutes)

- [ ] Get on the roof — Ember trailers have a walkable roof (verify with dealer before walking)
- [ ] Walk the full roof — inspect every penetration: A/C unit bases, vent collars, antenna, solar panels (if equipped), MaxxAir fan rings
- [ ] **Stargazer skylight (221MSL):** press the entire perimeter seal; this is a large skylight opening and the seal is critical — any lifting or gap must be repaired before delivery
- [ ] Press every sealant bead with fingernail — pliable = good; cracking = re-seal now
- [ ] **E-Series 26ETS Tufflex PVC roof:** one-piece PVC should be tight and smooth with no bubbles — check all four corners for any pulling or lifting
- [ ] **Photograph every sealant joint and the full roof surface — date-stamped before you leave the lot**

### Sidewalls and Seams

- [ ] Walk all four sides pressing along every seam, corner cap, and window trim
- [ ] Azdel walls will not "soft-spot" the way OSB-backed panels can, but check for any delamination bubbles — they can appear at impact points or improper seal areas
- [ ] Check all compartment doors: open, close, and latch each one flush
- [ ] Front fiberglass cap: any cracks at the A-frame hitch area or corners?

### Awning

- [ ] Deploy the full electric awning — it should extend smoothly with no hesitation or grinding
- [ ] Let it run fully extended, then walk the awning arm and rail for any misalignment
- [ ] Retract fully — awning motor failure is documented; test it twice
- [ ] **221MSL:** 18-foot awning — confirm it clears the slide when both are extended simultaneously

---

## Water System

- [ ] Confirm city water is connected before starting
- [ ] Open every faucet: kitchen, bathroom sink(s), shower, outside shower if equipped
- [ ] Let each run 2 minutes; look under every cabinet simultaneously
- [ ] Flush all toilets — bowl clears cleanly, no continuous running after flush
- [ ] Test fresh water pump: switch to tank water, prime, verify pressure
- [ ] Run tank flush (black tank rinse) if equipped — verify correct directional flow
- [ ] **Drain valve test:** pull each handle (black, gray) and confirm the labeled tank drains — mislabeled valves have been found across Elkhart brands
- [ ] Low-point drains: locate and confirm they close fully

---

## EmberTrack System — Test All Configurations

- [ ] Remove and reinstall at least one EmberTrack component — verify it locks in and doesn't shift laterally
- [ ] Configure bunks (if bunk kit is included): lock upper and lower, climb on each, apply lateral force
- [ ] Verify 300-lb per-position rating hasn't been compromised by incorrect bracket installation
- [ ] Test the desk configuration if included in your package
- [ ] Verify the rear cargo space is truly open (no fasteners left from factory that prevent full removal)

---

## Ground Control TT3 Auto-Leveling — Touring Edition 2500RDL Only

- [ ] Have dealer park on genuinely uneven ground before running this test
- [ ] Run a full auto-level cycle from scratch — all five points should extend, system should confirm level, no errors
- [ ] Download EmberLink app before you arrive — verify your phone connects to the trailer's system
- [ ] Run manual override via app: manually extend and retract each jack from the app
- [ ] Ask the dealer for the manual override procedure in case the app or the system fails in the field
- [ ] If any "battery low" error appears with a full charge — this is the same Ground Control calibration issue documented on Outdoors RV Titanium; ask the dealer to run the re-zero procedure before you sign

---

## Electrical and Inverter

- [ ] Test every 12V outlet and USB charging panel
- [ ] Test every 120V outlet with a plug tester
- [ ] GFCI test (per Critical section above)
- [ ] Verify battery bank state of charge — all batteries at equal charge
- [ ] Check inverter/charger display — no error codes or fault indicators
- [ ] If solar is equipped: verify MPPT controller shows PV input and charging current on a sunny day
- [ ] **Inverter failure is documented on Ember units** — if the inverter shows any fault code, require it to be resolved before signing

---

## Appliances

- [ ] **Refrigerator:** 4+ hours pre-run; verify below 40°F with thermometer before signing (12V compressor fridge on 221MSL — should be cold but may be louder than residential fridge)
- [ ] **Microwave / Air Fryer Convection (221MSL):** heat a cup of water for 60 seconds in microwave mode; then test air fryer mode
- [ ] **Cooktop:** light all burners, clean ignition, all hold flame
- [ ] **Furnace:** run full heat cycle — confirm warm air at all registers; check for spider nests in orifices
- [ ] **A/C:** run each unit 5 minutes, feel for cold air at every vent
- [ ] **Water heater:** full run cycle, verify hot at kitchen and bath

---

## Before You Sign — Non-Negotiables

- [ ] **Signed PDI form** with all findings documented
- [ ] **GFCI circuit verified** — dual appliance test, correct trip pattern
- [ ] **All PEX connections dry** under pressure at every sink, verified with you watching
- [ ] **All stabilizer legs deploy and retract** — or auto-leveling cycle complete (2500RDL)
- [ ] **Slide floor edge seal intact** and no swelling
- [ ] **All EmberTrack components locked** and stable under force
- [ ] **Awning deployed and retracted** twice without hesitation
- [ ] **Stargazer skylight seal pressed** — no lifting (221MSL)
- [ ] **Dated photos of every exterior seal** before the unit moves
- [ ] **Full 2-year warranty document** in hand
- [ ] **EmberLink app connected** and auto-leveling verified (2500RDL)
- [ ] **Service department direct contact** — name, phone, email — not the sales desk
- [ ] **Registered with Ember RV on delivery day** at emberrv.com

## Print-and-Bring Summary — 5 Critical Tests

1. **GFCI circuit**: run two appliances simultaneously, verify no trip, verify correct outlet pattern
2. **PEX water connections**: slow city water connect, 2 minutes under every sink with your face in the cabinet
3. **Stabilizer legs / auto-leveling**: all legs deploy fully; push test each corner (or full TT3 cycle on 2500RDL)
4. **Slide floor**: extend fully, press edge seam all around, look for swelling or soft spots
5. **EmberTrack anchors**: push hard on every mounted component; climb on every bunk

**If any of these five fail: do not sign. Pre-signature issues are the dealer's cost. Post-signature issues are warranty claims.**
