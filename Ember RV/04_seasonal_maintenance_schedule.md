# Ember RV — Seasonal Maintenance Schedule
*Overland 221MSL · E-Series 26ETS · Touring Edition 2500RDL*
*Date: 2026-05-23*

---

## What Makes Ember Maintenance Specific

Ember uses better base materials than standard Elkhart, but that changes the maintenance procedures in specific ways:

- **Azdel Onboard® composite walls** — no delamination risk from moisture penetration (unlike OSB-backed walls). Seam and cap sealants still need annual inspection, but the failure mode from a missed seal is less catastrophic than with OSB.
- **Tufflex® PVC roof (E-Series/Touring Edition)** — different care than standard TPO. PVC-specific cleaner and protectant recommended; Dicor lap sealant is still used at all penetration points.
- **One-piece Tufflex is not invincible** — the sealant at every penetration point (A/C bases, vents, solar brackets, antennas) still degrades with UV. Annual inspection and re-sealing applies.
- **EmberTrack™ system** — the E-Track hardware and mounting brackets need a quick annual inspect for any corrosion, loosening, or wear at locking points.
- **CURT Touring Coil Suspension (2500RDL)** — coil springs don't require greasing like MorRyde wet bolts, but annual inspection of mount bushings and shock absorbers applies.
- **Ground Control TT3 auto-leveling (2500RDL)** — same annual calibration check as Outdoors RV Titanium. Screw lubrication, connection inspection, re-zero procedure if errors occur.
- **Standard Dexter leaf spring (221MSL/26ETS)** — standard wheel bearing and brake schedule.
- **EmberLink app** — software updates; verify compatibility with your device each season.

## Before Every Trip (15 minutes)

- [ ] Walk all four exterior sides — any new sealant gaps, separation, or delamination bubbles since last trip?
- [ ] Check all compartment door latches — close and latch each one
- [ ] Run the EmberTrack system: if any component was reconfigured since last trip, lock and push-test each anchor point
- [ ] Run all slides out and back in once — confirm smooth operation before leaving
- [ ] Verify breakaway cable is connected, all lights working with tow vehicle connected
- [ ] Tire pressure cold — verify against spec for your loaded weight
- [ ] **2500RDL:** open EmberLink app, confirm system connection, verify auto-leveling shows no errors in standby

---

## After Every Trip (15 minutes)

- [ ] Walk exterior — note any new seal concerns, corner cap damage, or road debris impact
- [ ] Clean debris from slide rubber wiper seals before it sits
- [ ] Log any interior issues while fresh — what rattled, what didn't work, anything loose
- [ ] If the trailer sat in rain: check interior ceiling, slide floor edges, and all window seals for any wet spots
- [ ] After any rough road or off-road use: check EmberTrack anchor points — vibration can loosen bracket seating

## Spring / Season Open (3–4 hours)

### Roof — Annual Inspection and Re-Sealing

Ember's roof sealant degrades the same as any RV roof — UV exposure cracks and crumbles Dicor lap sealant regardless of what the roof membrane itself is made of.

- [ ] **Get on the roof** — walkable; use a 6-foot ladder
- [ ] Inspect every penetration sealant joint: A/C base(s), MaxxAir fan rims, vent collars, antenna(s), solar bracket(s) if equipped
- [ ] **221MSL Stargazer skylight:** press the full perimeter seal — it's a large opening and the seal is critical
- [ ] **Tufflex roof (26ETS/Touring):** check for any punctures, UV discoloration, or pulling at corners — the material is more durable than TPO but not immune to physical damage
- [ ] Press every sealant bead with fingernail — cracking or pulling = re-apply now
- [ ] Use **Dicor 501LSW** on horizontal surfaces; **Dicor 505LSW** on vertical seams
- [ ] **Photograph every sealant joint, date-stamped** — warranty baseline

### Sidewalls and Seams

- [ ] Walk all four sides, press along every vertical seam, corner cap, and window frame
- [ ] Azdel panels: check for any impact damage, stress cracks, or seam separation at panel edges
- [ ] Front fiberglass cap seam: press the full seam where cap meets sidewall — common separation point on any trailer
- [ ] All compartment door seals: close each door and look for any daylight or gap

### EmberTrack System — Annual Inspect

- [ ] Check all E-Track rail sections for any corrosion or debris in the channels
- [ ] Slide each mounted component out and back in — confirm it seats and locks cleanly
- [ ] Inspect all anchor bracket hardware for any loosening or wear at the lock points
- [ ] If any bracket shows wear or won't lock positively: contact Ember or your dealer for replacement hardware before use

### Slide Seals

- [ ] Extend all slides fully
- [ ] Inspect rubber wiper seals on all four sides of every slide — no tears, gaps, or separation
- [ ] Apply 303 Aerospace Protectant to all rubber slide seals
- [ ] Inspect slide membranes (top seals) for any lifting or tearing
- [ ] Clean slide tracks with a dry cloth; apply silicone-based dry lubricant (not petroleum-based)
- [ ] **26ETS:** re-inspect the slide floor edge seam — verify no new swelling or separation after winter storage

### De-Winterization

- [ ] Flush antifreeze from all lines — open every faucet until clear (pink antifreeze) or until no air burps (blow-out method)
- [ ] Install new water filter cartridge
- [ ] Pressurize city water — walk full trailer for 30 minutes under pressure
- [ ] **Water heater bypass:** return to normal before running hot water
- [ ] Test water pump on fresh tank
- [ ] Check all PEX connections after the winter freeze cycle — cold can cause fittings to shift

### Ground Control TT3 — Touring Edition 2500RDL

- [ ] Run a full auto-level cycle on uneven ground — verify all five points extend, system confirms level, no errors
- [ ] If any "batteries low" error occurs with full charge: run the Lippert re-zero procedure (available from Lippert at lci1.com and should be printed and kept in the trailer)
- [ ] Check all five leveling jack legs for corrosion or binding when retracted
- [ ] Lubricate leveling jack screws with dry lubricant — not grease (grease attracts dirt)
- [ ] Verify EmberLink app is updated and connects to the trailer

### Appliances — Annual Start-Up

- [ ] A/C units: run 20 minutes each, cold air at all vents — clean evaporator filter
- [ ] Furnace: run full heat cycle — **check cooktop orifices for spider nests before lighting**
- [ ] Light all cooktop burners, verify clean blue flame
- [ ] Water heater: run both electric and propane modes
- [ ] Refrigerator: 24 hours on electric, verify below 40°F
- [ ] **12V compressor fridge (221MSL):** verify compressor engages cleanly; listen for any unusual grinding — normal operation is a low hum

### Chassis and Tires

- [ ] Tire sidewall inspection: cracking, date codes (replace over 5–6 years)
- [ ] Cold tire pressure per spec — all Ember models run 15" tires on the Touring Edition; verify correct PSI for your configuration
- [ ] Lug nut torque — verify per spec
- [ ] Wheel bearing condition — spin each wheel, feel for roughness
- [ ] Brake inspection through wheel opening — pad wear, magnet condition

## Mid-Summer Check (1–2 hours)

- [ ] Roof spot-check — any seals that were borderline at spring open are the priority; summer UV accelerates degradation
- [ ] Re-condition slide rubber seals — second 303 Aerospace application
- [ ] **26ETS slide floor:** re-inspect edge seam mid-season if the trailer has been in heavy rain
- [ ] After any off-road or rough-road use: push-test all EmberTrack anchor points
- [ ] **2500RDL:** run auto-leveling cycle to verify system maintains calibration through season

---

## Fall / Season Close (3–4 hours)

### Final Seal Inspection Before Winter

- [ ] Full roof inspection — photograph and log, same protocol as spring open
- [ ] Re-seal any compromised joints before winter — moisture under ice is the worst sealant failure scenario
- [ ] Walk all four sidewalls — any new separation at seams or corner caps?
- [ ] **Log your fall seal work: date, locations, product names, photos**

### Trailer Winterization

**Option 1 — Air Blow-Out:**
- [ ] Compressor at max 50 PSI at city water inlet via blow-out plug
- [ ] Open faucets from low points up: outside shower → bathroom → kitchen
- [ ] Flush toilet, hold handle open until dry air flows
- [ ] Drain all three tanks; open low-point drains during storage

**Option 2 — Non-Toxic Antifreeze:**
- [ ] Drain tanks; bypass water heater
- [ ] Run non-toxic pink RV antifreeze through until pink appears at every faucet
- [ ] Pour antifreeze directly into toilet, all drains, outdoor shower line
- [ ] Remove and store water filter inside

### EmberTrack System — Storage Position

- [ ] Choose the most appropriate EmberTrack configuration for storage: consider whether you want bunk/desk/cargo during the winter for storage in the rear
- [ ] Inspect all anchor points once more before closing up — cold storage can cause metal to contract slightly at connections
- [ ] If storing cargo in the EmberTrack space: verify the weight doesn't exceed the 600 lb total rating

### Battery and Electrical

- [ ] Connect smart battery maintainer (not trickle) — prevents sulfation during storage
- [ ] Verify solar is clean for passive winter charging if trailer is stored outdoors
- [ ] Update EmberLink app before winter — run any available software updates while connected

### Exterior and Tires

- [ ] Apply 303 Aerospace Protectant to all rubber exterior moldings, entry door seal, slide seals before retraction
- [ ] Cover tires with UV-rated covers if storing outdoors
- [ ] Cover A/C shrouds — prevents debris and nesting

## Annual Service Items (Every 12 Months)

- [ ] Wheel bearing inspection and repack (packed bearings) or inspection (sealed)
- [ ] Brake inspection — pad wear, magnet condition
- [ ] Lug nut torque — re-torque after first 50 miles of season and at 12 months
- [ ] Smoke detector battery replacement
- [ ] CO and propane detector test — replace if past rated service life (5–7 years)
- [ ] Fire extinguisher charge check
- [ ] A/C coil cleaning — spray coil cleaner (not pressure water)
- [ ] Ground Control TT3 calibration check (2500RDL)
- [ ] EmberLink app update (any available firmware or software)
- [ ] EmberTrack hardware inspect — rails, brackets, lock points

## The Maintenance Log

One entry per event:
1. Date
2. What you inspected
3. What you found (including "nothing to report")
4. What you did (product name, specific location)
5. Photos attached

Ember's 2-year warranty covers workmanship and components. A dated maintenance log with roof seal photos demonstrates "normal maintenance" which protects you if Ember disputes a water damage claim on grounds of "lack of maintenance." The smaller Ember dealer network makes a clean maintenance record even more valuable if you ever need a non-dealer service center to handle a repair.
