## Brand at a Glance

| | Ember RV |
|---|---|
| **Builder** | Ember Recreational Vehicles |
| **Build location** | Goshen, Indiana |
| **Founded** | ~2019 — one of Elkhart's newer premium brands |

| **Auto-leveling** | Ground Control® TT3 with integrated electric stabilizers (Touring Edition standard) |
| **Signature feature** | **EmberTrack™** — patent-pending repositionable E-Track bunk/cargo system, configurable as bunks, desk, or open storage |
| **Tow vehicle** | Half-ton capable (221MSL/26ETS) · Verify per floor plan |
| **Price range** | ~$54K–$82K+ |
| **Warranty** | 2-year limited base — better base coverage than Alliance (1-yr) or Grand Design (1-yr base) |
| **Annual maintenance** | ~$160–185/year |

Ember entered the market positioning itself above standard Elkhart construction — Azdel walls, PVC roofing, coil suspension, integrated tech — at prices reflecting that premium. The EmberTrack modular system is genuinely innovative: the rear cargo/bunk space converts between sleeping, working, and hauling configurations without buying separate accessories. The Touring Edition adds Outdoors-RV-adjacent features (auto-leveling, coil suspension, dual-pane windows) at a price between Grand Design and ORV.

> **The honest positioning:** Ember is for the buyer who wants better materials than standard Elkhart (Azdel vs. OSB), a tech-forward interior, and genuine modularity — and who's willing to pay for it. The brand is newer, the dealer network is smaller, and warranty response has had documented delays. Owner communities are Facebook-based rather than dedicated forums, so pre-purchase research is harder. The construction materials are legitimately better than Grand Design's; the build quality execution is less consistent.

## ⚠️ 5 Documented Factory Defects — Verify at PDI

Documented across [RVForums.com](https://rvforums.com/threads/ember-brand-travel-trailers.14026/), [iRV2](https://www.irv2.com/), and [Ember RV Fan Club](https://www.facebook.com/groups/emberrvfanclub/) Facebook group:

| # | Defect | Test |
|---|---|---|
| 🔴 1 | **GFCI circuit miswired** — 20A GFCI breaker trips immediately when anything is plugged in (same wiring error as Alliance); documented on multiple units | Plug into every interior outlet with a lamp or phone charger; trip and reset the master GFCI, verify correct outlets respond |
| 🔴 2 | **PEX water connection leaks** — braided PVC connections at bathroom faucet hot/cold lines loosen in transit; documented on delivery day | Full pressurized walk under city water before signing; look inside every cabinet under every sink |
| 🔴 3 | **Stabilizer leg wiring** — crimp connectors not fully stripped at factory; leg won't deploy electrically | Extend all stabilizer legs electrically; push on each leg to verify it's fully extended and locked |
| 🔴 4 | **Slide-out floor water intrusion** — painted plywood slide floor swelled on documented 26ETS units; water tracks around fiberglass edge during rain | Inspect slide floor edges, look for swelling, soft spots, or paint bubbling; check floor-to-wall seam all around |
| 🔴 5 | **EmberTrack component installation** — shower control fixtures, cabinet doors, and E-Track anchor points installed incorrectly or misaligned on some units | Open/close every cabinet; test shower fixture direction; push on every EmberTrack anchor point |

## Warranty — 2-Year Base + Structural

| Coverage | Duration |
|---|---|
| Full unit (workmanship + components) | **2 years** (better than Alliance or GD's 1-year base) |
| Structural | Covered under base warranty (verify current document) |
| Transferability | Verify current warranty document — not confirmed as fully transferable |

**What to know:**
- The 2-year base warranty beats Grand Design's 1-year and Alliance's 1-year on raw duration
- Warranty repair approval delays are documented — dealers have reported difficulty getting authorization; one inverter replacement took 2+ months
- After base warranty expires, component claims go to respective manufacturers (Dometic, Furrion, Lippert, etc.) — same as all brands
- Smaller dealer network than Alliance or Grand Design — ask your dealer: *"What's your average Ember warranty turnaround, and do you stock common Ember parts?"*
