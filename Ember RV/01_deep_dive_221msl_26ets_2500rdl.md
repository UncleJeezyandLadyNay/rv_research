## Brand Context

Ember Recreational Vehicles launched around 2019 in Goshen, Indiana — neighboring Elkhart and sharing much of its supply chain — but positioned as a premium, design-forward alternative to the standard Elkhart playbook. Their differentiator is the **EmberTrack™ system**: a patent-pending repositionable E-Track framework that lets the rear cargo/bunk space convert between sleeping, working, and hauling configurations on the fly.

The brand built its lineup around three lines targeting different buyers:
- **Overland Series** — lighter, off-grid capable, premium price, half-ton towable
- **E-Series** — more affordable entry, still full Azdel construction, longer floor plans
- **Touring Edition** — premium all-rounder with coil suspension, auto-leveling, and the most amenities

**What Ember builds differently vs. standard Elkhart:**
- **Azdel Onboard® composite walls across the full lineup** — the same material Alliance Delta uses, better than Grand Design's OSB substrate. Fiberglass-reinforced polypropylene: no rot, no mold, no delamination.
- **Tufflex® PVC roof (E-Series)** — one-piece PVC with a manufacturer's lifetime warranty on the material itself (workmanship warranty still applies). Lighter and more UV-resistant than standard TPO.
- **CURT Touring Coil Suspension (Touring Edition)** — purpose-built coil spring suspension replacing standard leaf springs. Documented 50% road shock reduction. Goodyear Endurance 15" tires with TPMS standard.
- **Ground Control® TT3 auto-leveling (Touring Edition)** — 5-point auto-leveling system integrated with stabilizers. Controlled via the **EmberLink™ Smart RV app** — setup and leveling from your phone.
- **Evermore™ composite flooring (Touring Edition)** — Ember's proprietary composite subfloor with Congoleum LVP surface. No OSB, no plywood.

**What's standard Elkhart about Ember:**
- Aluminum frame construction (not steel like Outdoors RV)
- Lippert components throughout (axles, slides, hitching systems)
- Standard dealer delivery model with the same PDI challenges as any Elkhart brand
- Factory QC inconsistencies consistent with Goshen/Middlebury production volumes

## The EmberTrack™ System — What It Actually Is

Before looking at floor plans, understand the EmberTrack system because it defines the rear space on all three floor plans.

EmberTrack is a floor-to-ceiling E-Track framework (the same aircraft-cargo tie-down track system used in commercial logistics) anchored to Ember's frame. Into this track you can slide and lock components:
- **Bunk configuration**: two twin bunks, or one double bunk
- **Desk/work configuration**: fold-down surface with seating
- **Open cargo**: remove components entirely and use the space for gear, bikes, kayaks, or equipment hauling

All components lock into the track at defined positions. The system supports up to 600 lbs total (300 lbs per position). This is genuinely novel in the travel trailer segment — no other major brand offers a modular rear space that converts between bunks and cargo without tools or modification.

**The catch:** E-Track anchor points and the bracket hardware need to be verified at PDI. Misalignment or improper seating of brackets is a documented installation issue.

## Overland Series 221MSL

### Layout
The 221MSL puts a **Murphy bed** at the front (60" x 80" queen that folds against the wall during the day) and the EmberTrack system at the rear. The Murphy-up configuration opens the trailer into a single large living space; Murphy-down is a bedroom with the rear converted to additional sleeping. The "stargazer" skylight over the bed position is a signature Overland feature.

The single slide expands the main living area. When retracted for travel, the interior is fully walkable front to rear.

### Specifications (2025–2026)

| Spec | Value |
|---|---|
| UVW (Dry Weight) | ~5,850 lbs |
| GVWR | 7,550 lbs |
| Hitch Weight | ~715 lbs |
| Payload (cargo + people) | ~1,700 lbs |
| Overall Length | ~26 ft |
| Slides | 1 |
| Sleeps | 6 (Murphy bed + EmberTrack bunk configs) |
| Kitchen | 12V compressor fridge, dual burner cooktop, air fryer convection microwave |
| MSRP (2026) | ~$82,128 |

### Owner Sentiment

The 221MSL has the deepest owner history of the three floor plans. Overall sentiment is that the trailer is beautifully designed and the Murphy bed + EmberTrack concept works well in practice. Recurring complaints cluster around factory workmanship rather than design:

- Loose cabinets, exposed nails, and misaligned openings on some units at delivery
- One owner documented **19 warranty items** on their first 12-mile trip
- The 12V compressor refrigerator is praised for off-grid efficiency but documented to run loudly overnight

**The price conversation:** At ~$82K for a 26-foot trailer, the 221MSL costs as much as an Outdoors RV Titanium Timber Ridge 28BKS (which is a longer, heavier, 4-season built rig). The Ember delivers a lighter, half-ton-towable, tech-forward experience — but not 4-season capability, not steel frame construction, and not the ORV's off-road chassis. Whether $82K is right depends entirely on whether the ModularTrack modularity and half-ton tow capability are the priorities.

### Known Issues Specific to 221MSL

- Awning motor failure documented on some units — test the full awning deploy at PDI
- Stargazer skylight seal: inspect the rubber perimeter seal carefully; it's a large aperture in the roof
- EmberTrack brackets: push on all mounted components hard before signing; loose anchors are the most common installation defect

## E-Series 26ETS

### Layout
The 26ETS is a more conventional floor plan in Ember's more affordable line. Walk-around queen bed up front, kitchen and dinette in the middle, and the EmberTrack system filling the rear third. A single slide expands the main living area.

At 31'7" overall and 6,100 lbs dry, the 26ETS is longer and heavier than the 221MSL but costs nearly $30,000 less. This is the "value" entry into Ember's construction quality.

### Specifications (2025–2026)

| Spec | Value |
|---|---|
| UVW (Dry Weight) | ~6,100 lbs |
| GVWR | ~9,000 lbs (verify — not confirmed in available spec sheets) |
| Overall Length | 31' 7" |
| Slides | 1 |
| Sleeps | 4–6 (queen bed + EmberTrack bunk configs) |
| Roof | One-piece Tufflex® PVC (lifetime material warranty) |
| Exterior | Gel-coated fiberglass |
| MSRP (2026) | ~$53,659 |

### Owner Sentiment

The 26ETS is Ember's most price-competitive floor plan against the Grand Design Imagine and Alliance Delta. At ~$53-54K with Azdel walls and a Tufflex roof, it offers materially better construction than Grand Design Imagine at a similar price point.

Documented issues on the 26ETS specifically:
- **Slide-out floor water intrusion** — one documented case where the painted plywood slide floor swelled, allowing water to track around the fiberglass edge during rain. Inspect slide floor edges carefully at PDI.
- **PEX fitting leaks** — documented on 26ETS delivery units; leaking hot/cold bathroom connections
- **GFCI miswiring** — same 20A GFCI error documented on 26ETS that mirrors the Alliance Delta defect

**The honest take:** The 26ETS is arguably the best-value floor plan in the Ember lineup. Azdel walls and a Tufflex PVC roof at ~$53K is better materials than you get from Grand Design at the same price. The EmberTrack rear makes it genuinely more versatile than competing bunkhouse floor plans. The QC inconsistencies are real but appear to be workmanship errors rather than design flaws — things caught and fixed on a good PDI.

### Known Issues Specific to 26ETS

- Slide floor: inspect for swelling at the edge seam; look for water marks or paint bubbling
- PEX water connections at bathroom faucet lines: get under pressure and have a dealer tech look with you
- The 31'7" length is longer than it looks in showroom photos — measure your campsite before buying

## Touring Edition 2500RDL

### Layout
The 2500RDL is a **new floor plan** in the reimagined Touring Edition lineup, positioned as a 2027 model year offering. It brings a rear **U-shaped dinette** (RDL = Rear Dinette Layout) — a large communal gathering space at the rear — with a **private front bedroom**. This is the couples/family entertainer layout similar to the Alliance 284RK's rear kitchen concept but with dining space instead of a kitchen at the rear.

The Touring Edition's real differentiators over the Overland and E-Series are significant:
- **CURT Touring Coil Suspension** — not available on any other Ember line
- **Ground Control TT3 auto-leveling** — 5-point leveling with integrated stabilizers, phone app controlled
- **Evermore™ composite flooring** — proprietary composite subfloor (no OSB, no plywood)
- **Dual-pane caravan windows** — thermal efficiency and noise reduction
- **82" interior ceiling height** — 6" taller than typical Elkhart travel trailers
- **Solid surface countertops**

| Auto-leveling | Ground Control® TT3, 5-point, EmberLink app controlled |

| Walls | Azdel Onboard® composite |

*The 2500RDL is a newly announced floor plan. Confirm all specifications with your dealer before purchase — production specs may differ from announcement specs.*

### Owner Sentiment

**Limited data available** — the 2500RDL is a newly introduced floor plan with no meaningful owner history at time of research. Dealer feedback on the broader reimagined Touring Edition has been positive, particularly on the coil suspension and EmberLink integration. Treat the 2500RDL as a first-year floor plan — the "shakedown" period applies.

**What to know as an early buyer:**
- The Touring Edition's coil suspension and Ground Control TT3 are proven components used in other applications (Lippert Ground Control is the same system in Outdoors RV Titanium)
- The EmberLink app is newer — software maturity should be verified with current owners before buying
- First-year floor plans at any brand historically have more PDI issues than second/third-year iterations

### Known Issues Specific to 2500RDL

Insufficient owner data — apply general Ember PDI protocol (see checklist) and treat as a first-year floor plan.

## Where Ember Fits vs. the Other Brands

| | Ember RV | Grand Design Imagine | Alliance RV Delta | Outdoors RV Titanium |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Price range | ~$54K–$82K+ | ~$62K–$70K | ~$50K–$75K | ~$70K–$100K+ |
| Wall material | Azdel ✅ | OSB ❌ | Azdel ✅ | Hardwood/plywood |
| Roof | Tufflex PVC / TPO | One-piece TPO | One-piece TPO | TPO |
| Suspension | Leaf / Coil (Touring) | Standard Dexter | Standard | MorRyde AllTrek CRE4000 |
| Auto-leveling | Touring Edition only | No | No | Standard (Titanium) |
| Tow vehicle | Half-ton (Overland/E-Series) | 3/4-ton | Half-ton | 3/4-ton |
| Off-road | No | No | No | Yes |
| Four-season | No | No | Basic | Genuine |
| Modularity | EmberTrack™ ✅ | None | None | None |
| Owner community | Facebook groups only | MyGrandRV.com (large) | Dedicated forum (large) | iRV2, IFish |
| Warranty | 2-yr base | 1-3-5 transferable | 1-yr, exclusion trap | Standard |
| Factory QC | Inconsistent | Mixed | Recurring defects | Component errors only |

**The community bottom line on Ember:** Buyers who've done deep research on Ember are split — those who love it point to the genuinely better materials (Azdel vs. OSB, Tufflex vs. TPO), the EmberTrack innovation, and the 2-year warranty. Critics point to premium pricing for Elkhart execution quality, warranty approval delays, and a Facebook-only community that makes problem-solving slower. The Touring Edition with coil suspension and auto-leveling is the most defensible choice if the price is right at your dealer.
