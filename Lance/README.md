## Brand at a Glance

Lance Camper was founded in 1965 in Lancaster, California — a western builder in an industry dominated by Elkhart, Indiana production. That origin matters: Lance historically brought California manufacturing standards to an industry where "good enough" is routine. In 2017 REV Group acquired Lance, and production has since expanded to Decatur, Indiana. California-built heritage plus growing Midwest capacity.

The result is a brand that sits a clear tier above most Indiana competitors in build quality — tighter fit and finish, Huck-bolted NXG frame, Azdel composite walls throughout the lineup — while costing more than equivalent Indiana builds. The trade-off buyers make: better bones, higher price, documented warranty support challenges.

## What Sets Lance Apart from Indiana Builders

### NXG Huck-Bolted Aluminum Frame
The NXG frame uses Huck bolts — mechanically fastened, vibration-resistant precision fasteners — rather than welds or screws. Huck bolts create a permanent deformation-resistant joint that holds through years of road vibration. Competitive frames typically use self-tapping screws or basic weld points that can loosen. This is a genuine structural advantage, not marketing language.

### Azdel Composite Substrate — Standard Across the Lineup
Both the standard Lance line and the Evolve entry line use Azdel composite (not OSB) as the wall substrate. Azdel is:
- Rot- and mold-resistant (no wood pulp to absorb moisture)
- 50% lighter than wood at twice the strength
- Three times the insulating value of OSB
- Will not delaminate from moisture the way wood-substrate walls do

Indiana competitors using OSB — including Grand Design, Keystone, and others — have documented delamination failure modes that Azdel walls eliminate entirely.

### LanceLock™ Interlocking Aluminum Extrusions
All exterior panels connect via interlocking aluminum extrusions rather than screwed-on trim pieces. This is a structural connection, not cosmetic trim, and it distributes road stress across the panel joints rather than concentrating it at fasteners.

### Dexter Torsion Axles (Standard Lance Line)
The standard Lance line uses Dexter torsion axles rather than the leaf spring setups common on Indiana volume trailers. Torsion axles provide independent wheel movement, lower unsprung weight, and eliminate the leaf pack maintenance and noise associated with spring suspensions.

### Truma AquaGo Instant Hot Water (Most Models)
Lance standardized the Truma AquaGo on most models — including the 2465 and 2565. This is a genuine tankless water heater that produces unlimited hot water and dramatically reduces LP consumption compared to tank-style heaters. The trade-off: Truma requires annual descaling, and the unit is proprietary — Truma-certified service techs are not universal.

## The Flat Roof Problem — Know This Before You Buy

Lance uses a **flat TPO roof** — not a crowned or slightly pitched roof like many competitors. A flat roof ponds water rather than shedding it. When sealant at penetrations (A/C bases, vent collars, any mount points) fails, ponded water finds that opening and intrudes.

Per documented iRV2 forum threads, **Lance has declined to address roof ponding as a design defect**, characterizing sealant maintenance as the owner's responsibility. This isn't wrong — TPO sealant is an owner maintenance item on every brand. But on a flat roof, the ponded water is sitting on top of any sealant failure, not just running past it. The consequence of missed sealant maintenance is more immediate on a flat roof than on a crowned one.

**What this means for you:**
- Roof sealant inspection twice per season — not just annually
- More urgency than on a crowned roof when sealant shows cracking
- Check for evidence of ponding at delivery: look for the characteristic "tidemarks" of dried evaporated water on the flat roof surface

## The Two Lance Lines

### Standard Lance Travel Trailers (1475 through 2465)
Built to Lance's full spec: NXG Huck-bolted frame, full-body painted fiberglass exterior, Dexter torsion axles, Truma AquaGo hot water, Azdel composite walls. These are the trailers Lance has been making for decades. Higher price point, higher build quality.

### Lance Evolve Line
The Evolve is Lance's entry-level sub-brand, introduced to compete with Indiana pricing. Key differences from the standard line:
- Laminated fiberglass exterior (not full-body paint)
- Block foam insulation (vs. standard Lance insulation package)
- Entry-level cabinetry (Lite-Ply construction)
- Lower MSRP (~$30K–$50K range depending on model)
- Still uses Azdel composite substrate and aluminum frame construction
- Still built under Lance's manufacturing processes

The Evolve 2685 is the larger Evolve model — a step up in size and livability from entry Evolve units while remaining below the standard Lance price tier.

## 5 Documented Factory Defects

| # | Issue | Models Affected | What to Test at PDI |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Flat roof sealant voids at delivery** | All models | Walk the entire roof, press every sealant bead at every penetration — flat roof means zero tolerance for missed voids |
| 2 | **AC unit installation errors — leaks at roof penetration** | All models | Run both A/C units 10 min; have someone watch the ceiling and interior ceiling panels for any drip or staining |
| 3 | **Loose hardware, doors that won't open/close, split wood at delivery** | All models, iRV2-documented | Open and close every door and drawer; push on every component; check for quality before signing |
| 4 | **Backsplash buckling near gas stove** | All models with gas cooktop | Inspect the wall panel directly behind all gas burner positions before signing; press on it — soft = already separating |
| 5 | **Electrical problems at delivery** | All models | GFCI trip-and-reset test; run two 120V loads simultaneously; test every outlet |

## Warranty

**Lance Limited Warranty — 2 years total:**
- **Year 1:** Full warranty on most components within the coach — materials and workmanship
- **Year 2:** Structural only — walls, floors, and chassis

Component parts (electrical devices, propane appliances, furnaces, refrigerators, plumbing fixtures) are covered under their own manufacturer warranties, not Lance's.

**Important:** Lance warranty claim resistance is documented on PissedConsumer and BBB. Cases include Lance declining warranty work, instructing owners to drive to the factory and then not completing the promised work, and at least one documented case where Lance bought back a unit after legal pressure. Photograph every exterior seal and document every defect at PDI — it is your only leverage in a dispute.
