# Lance Travel Trailers — Maintenance Product List
*Evolve 2685 · Lance 2465 · Lance 2565*
*Date: 2026-05-24*

Products chosen for Lance's specific construction: flat TPO rubber roof, Azdel composite walls, NXG Huck-bolted aluminum frame, Truma AquaGo instant hot water (2465/2565), Dexter torsion axles (standard line).

## Sealants
*The flat TPO roof makes this the most critical category for Lance owners*

| Product | Use | Where to Buy | ~Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dicor 501LSW Self-Leveling Lap Sealant** (white) | All horizontal roof surfaces: A/C bases (full perimeter), vent collars, antenna mount, cable entries, front and rear wall-to-roof seams | Amazon, Camping World | ~$12/tube |
| **Dicor 505LSW Non-Sag Lap Sealant** (white) | Vertical: all sidewall seams, corner caps, window trim, door surround, front cap seam | Amazon, Camping World | ~$12/tube |
| **Dicor RP-RC-1 Rubber Roof Cleaner** | Annual clean of TPO roof before re-sealing — removes road grime and degraded sealant | Amazon, Camping World | ~$15 |
| **Dicor RP-CRP-1 Rubber Roof Conditioner** | UV protection for TPO membrane — apply after cleaning annually | Amazon, Camping World | ~$15 |
| **Eternabond RoofSeal Tape** (4" white) | Emergency field repair for any roof breach — bonds aggressively to TPO | Amazon, Camping World | ~$25/roll |

### Notes
- **Buy 3 tubes of 501LSW per season on a Lance** — the flat roof means you're using more sealant per inspection cycle than on a crowned roof. Don't let yourself run short mid-inspection.
- The front and rear wall-to-roof seams carry the most road flex stress — these are where voids appear first on Lance roofs
- Inspect twice per season minimum (spring and mid-summer); the flat roof removes the margin a pitched roof provides
- Keep 1 tube of each sealant in the trailer at all times for field repairs

## Truma AquaGo Maintenance — Lance 2465 and 2565

| Product | Use | Where to Buy | ~Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Truma Descaling Tablets** (or citric acid powder) | Annual descaling of the Truma AquaGo heat exchanger — prevents scale buildup that reduces efficiency and causes failure | Amazon, specialty RV suppliers, Truma directly | ~$15–$25 |
| **White wine vinegar** (5% acidity, 2 liters) | Alternative Truma descaling agent — 1:5 dilution with water; Truma-approved method | Any grocery store | ~$5 |
| **Truma Fault Code Reference Card** | Keep in the trailer — Truma error codes are cryptic without the reference; common codes (F0, F2, etc.) indicate specific issues | Download from truma.com, print and laminate | Free |

### Notes
- Descale annually minimum; every 6 months in hard water areas (over 300 ppm TDS)
- **Do not skip descaling.** Scale deposits on the heat exchanger insulate the element, force the unit to work harder, and lead to premature failure — repair or replacement of a Truma AquaGo is expensive and requires Truma-certified technicians
- Check your water TDS with a cheap meter (~$10) at any campground you frequent — hard water areas require more frequent descaling
- The Truma AquaGo is not winterized the same way as a tank heater — follow Truma's winterization procedure, not standard RV blow-out protocol

### Notes on Torsion Axles (2465/2565)
- Torsion axles do not have grease zerks and do not get repacked — they have sealed rubber torsion elements inside the axle tube
- Annual inspection = visual check of the axle tube for cracks, the end welds for integrity, and wheel bearing check via spin test
- If you hear or feel a change in ride quality, have a qualified RV tech inspect the torsion assembly

Tools and one-time items (ladder, torque wrench, tire gauge, plug tester, TDS meter) are first-year expenses only. The Lance annual maintenance cost is modestly higher than the Indiana field primarily because of the Truma descaling requirement — a trade-off most owners consider worthwhile given the Truma's performance advantage over tank water heaters. The flat roof's sealant demand (3 tubes instead of 2) is the other driver above the Indiana average.
