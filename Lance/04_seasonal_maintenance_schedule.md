# Lance Travel Trailers — Seasonal Maintenance Schedule
*Evolve 2685 · Lance 2465 · Lance 2565*
*Date: 2026-05-24*

---

## What Makes Lance Maintenance Specific

Lance's construction choices affect the maintenance schedule in specific ways:

- **Flat TPO roof** — the most important differentiator. A flat roof ponds water; every sealant void is a direct water intrusion path rather than a drip point. Inspection frequency should be higher than on a crowned roof. Lance's warranty language puts seal maintenance responsibility on the owner.
- **Azdel composite walls** — rot and mold resistant; the wall substrate itself doesn't need moisture protection. However, the exterior seam sealants still require annual inspection — any breach lets moisture into the laminated panel from the edge.
- **NXG Huck-bolted frame** — essentially maintenance-free; no fastener re-torquing needed on the frame itself the way Indiana frames occasionally require.
- **Truma AquaGo instant hot water (2465/2565)** — requires annual descaling. This is unique to Lance (and a few other brands). Skip it and the heat exchanger builds scale, loses efficiency, and eventually fails.
- **Dexter torsion axles (standard line)** — sealed bearings; typically require less maintenance than packed bearings but are inspected (not repacked) annually.
- **Evolve 2685: tank-style water heater** — requires standard anode rod inspection annually, not Truma descaling.

## Before Every Trip (15 minutes)

- [ ] Walk all four exterior sides — any new seal gaps, delamination bubbles, or corner cap damage since last trip?
- [ ] Check all compartment door latches
- [ ] Propane tanks — adequate supply, regulator hose undamaged
- [ ] Run slides out and back in once — smooth operation confirmed before leaving
- [ ] Lights check: running, brake, turn signals with tow vehicle connected
- [ ] Verify breakaway cable connected
- [ ] Tire pressure cold — verify against spec for your loaded weight
- [ ] **2465:** Both slides out and back in; any grinding or hesitation since last use?

---

## After Every Trip (15 minutes)

- [ ] Walk exterior — note any new seal concerns or road debris impact
- [ ] Clean debris from slide rubber wiper seals before it sits
- [ ] Log any issues while fresh — rattles, leaks, anything that didn't work right
- [ ] If unit sat in rain: check interior for wet spots at ceiling, windows, or slide floor — **on a flat roof, any wet interior ceiling spot is a sealant emergency; do not wait to address it**
- [ ] After any rain: if possible, check the roof surface — note any accumulated standing water and where it pools

## Spring / Season Open (3–4 hours)
*Most important maintenance event of the year*

### Roof — Annual Inspection and Re-Sealing (Lance Priority #1)

Lance's flat roof means this is the most consequential maintenance task you perform. A crowned roof gives you a season of margin when a sealant bead cracks; a flat roof does not.

- [ ] **Get on the roof** — walkable TPO; 6-foot ladder
- [ ] Walk the full roof; press every sealant bead at every penetration: A/C bases (full perimeter, not just the front), all vent collars, MaxxAir fans, antenna mount, any cable entry points
- [ ] **Front and rear wall-to-roof seams** — press the full length of each; these carry the most road flex stress
- [ ] Press each bead firmly with your fingernail: pliable = intact; cracking = re-seal with Dicor 501LSW immediately
- [ ] Note any tidemarks (dried water rings) that indicate pooling spots — mark these locations for extra vigilance
- [ ] Both A/C covers: no new cracking, all mounting bolts present
- [ ] **Photograph every sealant joint with date stamp — warranty baseline documentation for the year**

### Sidewalls and Exterior Seams

- [ ] Walk all four sides; press every vertical seam, corner cap, and window frame
- [ ] Re-seal any gaps or cracks with Dicor 505LSW non-sag (vertical surfaces)
- [ ] Front cap seam: press the full seam where the fiberglass meets the sidewall
- [ ] Check LanceLock™ extrusion channels — press along the panel edge connections; any separation of the extrusion from the panel requires attention

### Slide Seals

- [ ] Extend all slides
- [ ] Inspect rubber wiper seals: no tears, no gaps, no UV cracking — all four sides of every slide
- [ ] Apply 303 Aerospace Protectant to all rubber slide seals
- [ ] Inspect slide membranes (top seals) — any lifting or tearing?
- [ ] Clean slide tracks with a dry cloth; apply Camco Slide-Out Dry Lube (silicone-based only)
- [ ] Retract slides; verify smooth operation

### De-Winterization

- [ ] Flush antifreeze from all lines — open every faucet until clear (pink method) or air-free (blow-out method)
- [ ] Install new water filter cartridge
- [ ] Pressurize system on city water for 30 minutes; walk every cabinet and connection
- [ ] **Water heater bypass: return to normal position before running hot water**
- [ ] Test water pump on fresh tank

### Truma AquaGo Inspection and Descaling (2465/2565)

The Truma AquaGo is one of Lance's best features and one of its most specific maintenance requirements. Truma recommends descaling:
- **Annually** for most owners
- **Every six months** in hard water areas (total dissolved solids over 300 ppm)
- After approximately every 1,585 gallons of use

**Spring descaling procedure:**
- [ ] Confirm unit is off and cool
- [ ] Prepare descaling solution: Truma-approved descaling agent, or white wine vinegar diluted 1:5 (2 liters vinegar to 10 liters water); **citric acid (1 tablespoon per liter) also works**
- [ ] Connect descaling solution per Truma's procedure (inlet fitting)
- [ ] Run the cycle; flush with fresh water until no vinegar/citric acid smell remains
- [ ] Run a full hot water cycle to verify the unit fires correctly and heats to temperature

Signs that descaling is overdue: longer time to hot water, reduced flow, Truma error codes, increased propane consumption for hot water.

### Anode Rod Inspection — Evolve 2685 (Tank Water Heater)

- [ ] Drain the tank water heater fully
- [ ] Remove the anode rod; inspect for significant depletion (less than 25% magnesium remaining = replace)
- [ ] Flush the tank to remove sediment before refilling

### Appliances — Annual Start-Up

- [ ] Both A/C units: run 20 minutes each, cold air at all vents — clean or replace evaporator filter
- [ ] Furnace: full heat cycle — **check cooktop orifices and furnace intake for spider/wasp nests; seasonal storage is prime time for nesting**
- [ ] Light all cooktop burners; verify clean blue flame on each
- [ ] Refrigerator: 24 hours on electric; verify below 40°F

### Chassis and Tires

- [ ] Tire sidewall inspection: cracking, date codes (replace over 5–6 years regardless of tread)
- [ ] Cold tire pressure per spec — all four plus spare if equipped
- [ ] Lug nut torque — verify per spec (typically 90–100 ft-lbs; confirm for your axle)
- [ ] **Torsion axle inspection (2465/2565):** Visually inspect torsion axle tube and equalizer for any cracks, wear, or fluid leaks; torsion axles don't get repacked but do get inspected
- [ ] Wheel bearing inspection: spin each wheel; feel for roughness or resistance
- [ ] Brake inspection through wheel opening: pad wear, magnet condition

## Mid-Summer Check (1–2 hours)

- [ ] **Roof spot-check — priority on Lance** — summer UV heat accelerates sealant degradation; any bead that was borderline at spring open may crack by July. The flat roof makes this urgent.
- [ ] Re-condition slide rubber seals — second 303 Aerospace application
- [ ] After any heavy rain: walk the interior ceiling for any wet spots at the A/C mounts, vent locations, or wall-to-roof seams — flat roof means water can pool and find voids faster than on a pitched roof
- [ ] Truma AquaGo: no error codes, hot water within 10 seconds — if it's getting slower, descaling may be needed mid-season

---

## Fall / Season Close (3–4 hours)

### Final Seal Inspection Before Winter

- [ ] Full roof inspection — photograph and log same as spring
- [ ] Re-seal any compromised joints before winter — standing ice on a flat roof bearing on a sealant void is worst-case
- [ ] Walk all four sidewalls — any new separation?
- [ ] **Log fall seal work: date, locations, product names, photos**

### Trailer Winterization

**Option 1 — Air Blow-Out:**
- [ ] Compressor at max 50 PSI at city water inlet via blow-out plug
- [ ] Open faucets from low points up; hold each open until dry air flows
- [ ] Drain all three tanks; leave low-point drain valves open during storage
- [ ] **Truma AquaGo (2465/2565):** The Truma has a specific winterization procedure — consult the Truma manual. Do not blow out the Truma with full compressor pressure without following Truma's protocol; the unit can be damaged by over-pressure.

**Option 2 — Non-Toxic Antifreeze:**
- [ ] Drain all tanks; bypass water heater
- [ ] **Truma bypass:** Confirm the Truma bypass valve is engaged before running antifreeze — the Truma should not have antifreeze run through it; it has its own winterization procedure
- [ ] Run pink antifreeze through lines until pink appears at every faucet
- [ ] Pour antifreeze into toilet and all drains
- [ ] Remove and store water filter inside

### Truma AquaGo Winterization (2465/2565)

The Truma AquaGo requires its own winterization separate from the main system:
- [ ] Follow Truma's manual winterization procedure — the unit has a draining sequence
- [ ] Do not attempt to blow out the Truma with a compressor; use only the Truma-approved procedure
- [ ] Verify the Truma drain indicator shows fully drained before winter storage

### Battery and Electrical

- [ ] Connect smart battery maintainer (not trickle) during storage
- [ ] Disconnect battery if storing in extreme cold — bring inside if possible
- [ ] Cover A/C shroud(s) — prevents debris and rodent nesting

### Exterior and Tires

- [ ] Apply 303 Aerospace to all rubber moldings and exterior seals before storage
- [ ] Cover tires if storing outdoors — UV tire covers
- [ ] Move trailer monthly during storage to prevent flat spots

## Annual Service Items (Every 12 Months)

- [ ] **Truma AquaGo descaling (2465/2565)** — if not done in spring, do it now
- [ ] **Anode rod inspection — Evolve 2685** — tank water heater only
- [ ] Wheel bearing inspection (torsion axles — inspect, don't repack)
- [ ] Brake inspection — pad wear, magnet condition
- [ ] Lug nut torque — re-torque after first 50 miles of season and at 12 months
- [ ] Smoke detector battery replacement
- [ ] CO and propane detector test — replace if past rated service life (5–7 years)
- [ ] Fire extinguisher charge check
- [ ] A/C coil cleaning — spray coil cleaner; never pressure wash the coils directly
- [ ] LanceLock™ extrusion channel inspection — full perimeter of all four walls

## The Maintenance Log

One entry per event:
1. Date
2. What you inspected
3. What you found (including "nothing to report")
4. What you did (product name, specific location)
5. Photos attached

**Critical for Lance specifically:** Lance's warranty claim documentation requirement is real. The flat-roof design means water damage claims will be scrutinized for sealant maintenance records. A dated maintenance log with roof photos from each season — showing which penetrations you inspected, what you found, and what product you applied — is the evidence that makes "inadequate seal maintenance" an untenable argument against a legitimate claim. Keep photos of every sealant bead you apply.
