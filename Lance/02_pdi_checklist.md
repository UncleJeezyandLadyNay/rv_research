# Lance Travel Trailers — PDI Checklist
*Evolve 2685 · Lance 2465 · Lance 2565*
*Date: 2026-05-24*

Print this. Bring it to the dealership. Do not sign until every item is checked.

**Time budget: 3–4 hours.** Lance dealers are fewer than Keystone or Grand Design dealers — this can be a good or bad sign depending on the specific dealer. Ask when booking: "How many Lance units does your service department deliver per month?" A dealer moving 5+ Lance units monthly will have techs who know the flat-roof sealant check and the A/C installation issue. A dealer who treats Lance as a secondary line may not.

**The PDI matters on Lance.** Issues found before signing are the dealer's cost. Issues found after signing are warranty claims — and Lance warranty claim friction is documented. Your strongest position is at the PDI, before keys change hands.

## 🔴 CRITICAL — Factory-Documented Defects (Start Here)

### 1. Roof Sealant Inspection — All Models
**Known issue:** Lance's flat TPO roof ponds water rather than shedding it. Sealant voids at any roof penetration — A/C bases, vent collars, antenna mount, cable entry points — allow ponded water to intrude directly. Voids have been found at delivery.

- [ ] Get on the roof — Lance TPO roofs are walkable; use a 6-foot ladder
- [ ] Walk the entire roof surface — look for any debris, existing staining, or tidemarks (rings from evaporated standing water indicate the roof ponds heavily)
- [ ] Press every sealant bead with your fingernail at every penetration: A/C base perimeter, all vent collars, MaxxAir fans, antenna mount, any cable entry points, the front and rear wall-to-roof seams
- [ ] Any sealant that cracks, flakes, or shows visible voids = require re-application with Dicor 501LSW before signing
- [ ] Photograph every sealant joint — date-stamped — this is your warranty baseline
- [ ] **Note the roof flatness:** Look across the roof from end to end. A genuinely flat roof will show no crown. This is by design; you need to know it before you take ownership

---

### 2. A/C Unit Installation — All Models
**Known defect:** Brand-new Lance travel trailers have arrived at owners' homes with incorrectly installed A/C units that leaked through the roof penetration or the interior ceiling pan. The installation error is at the factory, not the dealer.

- [ ] Run both A/C units (one if single A/C model) for a minimum of 10 minutes each
- [ ] While A/C is running: have someone inside watch the ceiling panel around the A/C mounting location for any dripping or water staining
- [ ] Check the roof surface around each A/C base: gasket fully seated, base mounting bolts present and snug, sealant bead fully applied around the entire perimeter with no gaps
- [ ] If any ceiling panel shows moisture or the gasket appears improperly seated: **require inspection and repair before signing**

---

### 3. Hardware and Fit Quality — All Models
**Known defect:** Documented iRV2 reports of loose screws, split wood, doors that won't open or close properly, and electrical problems found on Lance units at delivery. This is not a rumor — it's a consistent thread in owner forums.

- [ ] Open every cabinet door and drawer in the unit — every single one
- [ ] Each door should: open smoothly, close fully flush, latch securely without rattling
- [ ] Each drawer should: pull out on its slides without binding, close flush
- [ ] Look inside each cabinet: check that hinges are fully secured, no screws backed out
- [ ] Push on every wall-mounted component: TV bracket, shelving, paper towel holders, towel bars — any movement means the mount is not properly fastened
- [ ] Check bunk ladder hardware if applicable (Evolve 2685): all bolts tight, no flex in the ladder structure

---

### 4. Backsplash Near Gas Stove — All Models with Gas Cooktop
**Known defect:** Documented reports of the wall panel material behind gas burners buckling from inadequate heat shielding. The material separates from the wall structure after heat exposure.

- [ ] Before running the stove: press firmly on the wall panel directly behind all burner positions — should be rigid, flush, firmly bonded
- [ ] Any softness, bubbling, or visible separation = the panel has already begun to delaminate — require repair before signing
- [ ] Light all burners and run for 2 minutes; verify clean blue flames on all burners
- [ ] After running burners: press the backsplash again; note any warmth pattern that extends beyond what would be expected from a properly shielded surface

---

### 5. Electrical System — All Models
**Known issue:** Electrical problems documented at delivery on new Lance units — including GFCI wiring errors and outlet issues.

- [ ] Test every 120V outlet with a 3-prong plug tester
- [ ] Test every 12V outlet and USB charging point
- [ ] Identify the GFCI master outlet (typically bathroom or kitchen) — trip it, verify that the correct downstream outlets lose power, then reset and verify they restore
- [ ] Run the microwave and an A/C unit simultaneously — verify no circuit trips under dual load
- [ ] **2465:** Verify the electric fireplace operates on its own circuit — run it simultaneously with another 120V appliance

## Evolve 2685 — Specific Checks

- [ ] **Verify specs at delivery:** Confirm dry weight and GVWR with the dealer — Lance's Evolve 2685 spec sheet was not published on Lance's website at time of research. You need the actual weight to verify your tow vehicle payload capacity.
- [ ] **Tow vehicle payload check:** With GVWR confirmed, compare against your truck's payload rating (not just max tow — payload) to ensure you're not overloaded at the pin
- [ ] **Exterior wash station:** Run water through the exterior wash station; verify the fitting is properly sealed where it enters the trailer wall
- [ ] **Dual battery rack:** Verify both batteries are present, fully charged, and the dual setup is correctly wired (both drawing/charging equally)
- [ ] **Block foam insulation:** Check the underbelly is fully enclosed — no gaps in the belly wrap where block foam insulation is exposed

## Exterior Walk — All Models

### Roof (15 minutes)
- [ ] Walk the full roof — start at the front wall-to-roof seam, walk to rear, work the perimeter
- [ ] Press the front and rear seams especially — where the TPO roof material meets the front and rear cap
- [ ] Both A/C covers: no cracks, no broken clips, all mounting bolts present
- [ ] Photograph every penetration: date-stamped

### Sidewalls and Seams
- [ ] Walk all four sides; press every vertical seam, corner cap, window frame, and door surround
- [ ] Any soft spots in wall panels = moisture already present — stop the PDI and investigate
- [ ] All compartment doors: open, close, latch flush, Keyed-Alike operation confirmed

### Awning
- [ ] Full deployment; walk the arm and rail for misalignment
- [ ] Retract smoothly — no grinding, no hesitation

## Water System

- [ ] Shore water connected before starting this section
- [ ] Connect city water slowly — walk under pressure for 20 minutes minimum
- [ ] Open every faucet: kitchen, bathroom sink, shower
- [ ] Look under every sink cabinet for drips — press a paper towel against every fitting
- [ ] Run shower for 5 minutes — check pan for any flex underfoot, check door seal for any spray escaping the enclosure
- [ ] Flush toilet — clean bowl fill, no running after flush
- [ ] Verify dump valve labeling matches actual tanks (gray vs black)
- [ ] **Truma AquaGo (2465/2565):** Run the kitchen hot tap — water must go from cold to hot within 5–10 seconds of running; this is the Truma's design spec; if it takes 30 seconds to heat, the unit may not be firing correctly

---

## Appliances

- [ ] **A/C units:** Run each 10 minutes minimum; cold air at every vent; ceiling panels around A/C mounts showing no moisture (per Critical section)
- [ ] **Furnace:** Full heat cycle; warm air at all registers; **check furnace orifice and cooktop for spider/wasp nests — a common problem on new units from storage**
- [ ] **Cooktop:** All burners light cleanly, hold stable flame (no yellow tips)
- [ ] **Microwave:** 60-second water test
- [ ] **Water heater:** Hot within 10 seconds at kitchen tap (Truma); or verify tank heater reaches temp after 30 min (Evolve 2685 tank-style heater)
- [ ] **Refrigerator:** Must be below 40°F after 4+ hours on shore power before delivery — verify with thermometer

---

## Chassis and Underside

- [ ] Walk underside with flashlight: belly wrap fully intact; no sections hanging loose; all drain lines secured without kinks
- [ ] Torsion axle hanger bolts — all present, no visible looseness (standard 2465/2565)
- [ ] All running lights and brake lights operational with tow vehicle connected

## Print-and-Bring Summary — 5 Critical Tests

1. **Roof:** Walk it, press every sealant bead; photograph everything; flat roof = zero tolerance for voids
2. **A/C units:** Run both 10 minutes; watch for ceiling moisture around the mounting area
3. **All doors and drawers:** Every single one — open, close, latch; hardware tight
4. **Backsplash behind stove:** Press the wall panel behind all burners before and after running
5. **Electrical:** Test all outlets with a plug tester; GFCI trip and reset

**If any of the five fail: do not sign. Pre-signature = dealer's cost. Post-signature = warranty claim.**
