# Lance Travel Trailers — PDI Walkthrough Script
*Word-for-word script for use at the dealership*
*Evolve 2685 · Lance 2465 · Lance 2565*
*Date: 2026-05-24*

**Time budget: 3–4 hours.** Lance dealers vary significantly in experience. Some have delivered dozens of 2465s and know the flat-roof sealant priority and the A/C installation check. Others sell Lance as a secondary line alongside Jayco and Keystone and will push through your PDI as quickly as possible. Ask when booking: "How many Lance units does your service department deliver per month?"

---

## The Day Before — Call the Dealer

> *"I'm coming in tomorrow for my PDI on the [Evolve 2685 / Lance 2465 / Lance 2565]. Before I arrive, I need the unit on shore power with the refrigerator running for at least four hours. I also need the Truma water heater switched on at least 30 minutes before I arrive [skip for Evolve 2685 if equipped with tank heater — ask]. Plan for three to four hours. I'll be going on the roof, so I'll need a ladder."*

If they say they don't have time for a 3-4 hour PDI:

> *"I've read that Lance's warranty has a documented friction point on new-unit claims. I want to find everything now while it's your cost to fix, not mine. Three to four hours is what a Lance PDI takes done right."*

---

## Arrival — Set the Frame (5 minutes)

> *"I've done specific research on this model and I have a checklist. I want to go through everything systematically — three to four hours. I understand Lance builds a quality product and I'm excited about this one; I just want to make sure my specific unit came through correctly. Fair?"*

Then:

> *"Can I have a copy of the PDI form your team completed? And I'll need a signed copy at the end with anything we find today added to it."*

---

## Roof Walk — START HERE (15 minutes)

This is the most important inspection on any Lance trailer. The flat roof design means sealant maintenance is the difference between a dry interior and a water damage claim that Lance may contest.

> *"I want to start on the roof. I've read about Lance's flat-roof design — water ponds rather than sheds — and I want to verify every sealant joint before I look at anything else."*

On the roof:

> *"I'm going to press every sealant bead with my fingernail. If anything cracks, flakes, or separates, I need it re-applied with Dicor 501LSW before I sign."*

Work systematically from front to back:

> *"Starting at the front wall-to-roof seam. This is where the TPO meets the front fiberglass cap — it's a high-stress joint."*

Press the full length of the front seam. Then:

> *"I'm moving to the A/C base. Walking the entire perimeter gasket plus the sealant bead."*

Work through every vent, fan, antenna mount, and cable entry point:

> *"Every penetration gets pressed — all the way around, not just the top of the bead."*

If you find any void or cracking:

> *"This sealant has a void. On a flat roof, standing water sits directly on top of this — it will intrude at the first rain. I need this re-applied before I sign. This is Lance's documented highest water-intrusion risk."*

Photograph every sealant joint before leaving the roof:

> *"I'm photographing every sealant bead with a timestamp. This is my warranty baseline — if I ever have a water damage claim, this is the documentation that proves the unit was sealed correctly at delivery."*

---

## A/C Unit Inspection (10 minutes)

> *"I want to run both A/C units now, while I can still watch the ceiling from inside. I've read about a documented factory installation error where brand new Lance trailers had incorrectly installed A/C units that leaked at the roof penetration."*

Start both A/C units and run for 10 minutes. Go inside and watch:

> *"I want someone outside watching the roof around the A/C bases, and I'm watching the ceiling panels inside. Any drip, staining, or moisture around the A/C mount — that's the defect I'm looking for."*

If you see moisture:

> *"There's moisture showing at the ceiling panel around the A/C mount. This is the factory installation defect I was checking for. I need the A/C unit inspected and the roof penetration properly sealed before I sign."*

---

## Hardware and Fit Quality Walk (15 minutes)

> *"I want to go through every door, drawer, and cabinet. I've found iRV2 documentation of loose screws, split wood, and doors that don't close properly on new Lance units. This should only take 15 minutes."*

Open every door and drawer systematically:

> *"[Opening kitchen cabinet] — opens smoothly, closes flush, latch holds. [Continuing to next.] — opening and closing every cabinet in the unit."*

If you find a door that doesn't close flush or a drawer that binds:

> *"This drawer doesn't close flush — I can see the slide is misaligned. I want that adjusted before I sign."*

Push on every wall-mounted component:

> *"Checking every shelf, towel bar, TV bracket, paper towel holder — all should be solidly fastened."*

---

## Backsplash Inspection (5 minutes)

> *"Before I use the stove, I want to check the wall panel behind the burners. There are documented cases of the backsplash material buckling from heat exposure on new Lance units."*

Press the wall directly behind each burner position:

> *"This should be completely rigid and flush. Any softness or bubbling means it's already separating."*

If the panel feels soft or shows bubbling:

> *"The panel behind the burners has already started to delaminate. This is the factory defect I'm checking for. I need this repaired before I sign."*

Now light all burners:

> *"Running all burners briefly — looking for clean blue flames, no yellow tips. And I'll check the backsplash again after."*

---

## Water System and Truma Verification (15 minutes)

### For Lance 2465 and 2565 (with Truma AquaGo):

> *"Is the Truma water heater running? I need hot water at the kitchen tap within ten seconds of running. That's the Truma's design spec — if it's working correctly, it heats almost instantly."*

Run the kitchen hot tap:

> *"Counting to ten — should be hot by now. If it's not fully hot in ten seconds, the Truma isn't firing correctly."*

If the water doesn't heat within 10 seconds:

> *"The Truma isn't heating properly. This could be a gas pressure issue, an ignition fault, or an installation problem. I want a service tech to diagnose it before I continue."*

### For Evolve 2685 (with tank-style water heater):

> *"I asked you to start the water heater before I arrived. Let's run the hot tap and verify it's fully hot — not warm."*

### All Models — Full Water System Walk:

> *"I'll be checking under every sink cabinet while the water's on. Please walk with me and note anything that drips."*

Walk every cabinet, then:

> *"Running the shower for five minutes — checking the pan support, the door seal, and watching the base for any water escape."*

Step into the shower pan:

> *"Full weight, slight bounce — the pan should be completely rigid. Any flex means the support underneath is inadequate."*

---

## Slides Walk (10 minutes)

### Lance 2465 — Both Slides:

> *"Let's extend both slides."*

Watch each one:

> *"I want to see both slides go to full extension independently. Any grinding, hesitation, or error on the controller?"*

Then:

> *"Now let's extend both simultaneously and verify the controller handles both without errors."*

Walk the full seal perimeter of each extended slide:

> *"I'm pressing the rubber wiper seals on all four sides of each slide — checking for tears, gaps, or UV cracking."*

Retract:

> *"Retracting both — watching for smooth operation, no grinding."*

### Lance 2565 — Single Slide:

> *"Let's extend the slide."*

Watch it go to full extension. Walk the seals:

> *"Pressing all four sides of the wiper seal on the slide — full perimeter."*

---

## Electrical Walk (10 minutes)

> *"I have a plug tester — let's go through every outlet."*

Test every 120V outlet:

> *"[Testing kitchen outlets.] — correct wiring. [Testing bathroom outlet.] — GFCI protected. [Testing bedroom outlets.] — correct wiring."*

GFCI test:

> *"I'm tripping the GFCI master. Let me verify which outlets lose power — they should be the bathroom and any other GFCI-protected outlet chain. [Noting which outlets are affected.] Now resetting."*

Dual-load test:

> *"I'm running the microwave and the A/C simultaneously — verifying no circuit trips under dual load."*

---

## Model-Specific Checks

### Lance 2465:

> *"I want to test the electric fireplace on its own — running it for five minutes, verifying the remote functions, and making sure it's on a dedicated circuit."*

> *"The California King bed — I want to sit on it and check the structural support. And I'll open the under-bed storage."*

If motorized TV lift is equipped:

> *"Let me cycle the TV lift — full up and full down — and verify there's no binding."*

### Evolve 2685:

> *"I want the actual GVWR from your documentation — Lance doesn't publish the Evolve 2685 spec sheet on their website, and I need the confirmed GVWR in writing to verify against my tow vehicle payload."*

Do not accept verbal confirmation:

> *"I need this in writing on the PDI form — the actual GVWR of this specific unit."*

---

## Appliances (15 minutes)

> *"Let's work through all the appliances."*

- Both A/C units: already running from the earlier test — verify cold air at every vent
- Furnace: full heat cycle — **check cooktop burner area and furnace orifice for spider/wasp nests — common on units that have been in dealer storage**
- Cooktop: all burners, clean ignition
- Microwave: 60-second water test
- Refrigerator: already running 4+ hours — verify below 40°F with a thermometer you bring

---

## Final Questions Before Signing

> *"What's your typical turnaround time on Lance warranty parts? I know Lance warranty support has some documented friction — I want to understand what I'm working with if I file a claim in year one."*

> *"If I have a warranty issue, do you have a direct Lance rep you work with? Or does everything go through Lance's standard process?"*

> *"If any repair takes more than 30 days, what's your policy on loaner or reimbursement?"*

> *"Is this unit California-built or Indiana-built?" [REV Group now builds Lance in both states — knowing which affects expectations on some owners]*

---

## Walkthrough Wrap-Up

> *"Here's my complete list."*

State everything found. Then:

> *"Everything fixable today, I'd like fixed and retested before I sign. For anything that needs to be scheduled — seal repairs, A/C work, hardware fixes — I want a specific date in writing."*

If you hear "we'll handle it after delivery":

> *"Before I sign, these are your cost to fix without warranty approval delays. Once I drive away, each of these becomes a Lance warranty claim. I'm patient enough to come back when it's done. Can we set a date now?"*
