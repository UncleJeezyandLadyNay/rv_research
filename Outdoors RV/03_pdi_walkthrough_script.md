# Outdoors RV Titanium Series - PDI Walkthrough Script
*Word-for-word script for use at the dealership*
*Date: 2026-05-22*

**Time budget: plan for 3-4 hours.** Outdoors RV dealers who specialize in ORV tend to know the product well and respond positively to informed buyers. Come prepared and they'll respect the process.

---

## Before You Arrive

Call the dealer the day before:

> *"I want to make sure the unit is connected to shore power, city water is hooked up, and the refrigerator has been running at least four hours before I get there. I'd also like someone to start the generator and warm it up before my walkthrough. Can you set that up?"*

---

## Arrival - Set the Frame (5 minutes)

> *"I've done a lot of research on Outdoors RV and I have a specific list of things I want to go through today. I know ORV builds a great product — I just want to make sure my specific unit came through correctly. Fair?"*

Then immediately:

> *"Can I get a copy of the PDI form your team completed? And I'll need a signed copy at the end with anything we find today added."*

---

## Auto Leveling System — START HERE (15 minutes)

The Ground Control TT auto leveling is the Titanium's signature feature. It's also the one most likely to have a quirk at delivery.

> *"I'd like to start with the auto leveling system. Can you put the trailer on uneven ground and run a complete auto-level cycle from scratch?"*

Watch: all four jacks should extend, the system should settle and confirm level, and the display should show no errors. If it errors out:

> *"The system is showing an error. I've read that the Ground Control sometimes needs to be re-zeroed — can you walk me through that procedure? And can you show me where that's documented so I can do it in the field if needed?"*

If the system errors on "batteries low" while solar shows full:

> *"This is a known issue with the Ground Control on Titanium models — the system needs to be re-zeroed. Can we do that now and retest? I'd like it working before I take the unit."*

After it works:

> *"Can you show me the manual override procedure for leveling jacks if the auto system fails in the field?"*

---

## Generator — 26RPS and 28DBS (15 minutes)

> *"Let's start the generator. I want to run it for at least 10 minutes and load it with the A/C while it's running."*

Start the generator and let it stabilize. Then turn on the A/C. Listen for:
- Smooth, steady exhaust note — no misfires or roughness
- No voltage sag or shutdown under A/C load

Then ask the critical question:

> *"I've read that some BackCountry units shipped with the spark plug gap set at .028 instead of the specified .020. Can you pull one plug and show me the gap?"*

If the dealer says they already checked it:
> *"I appreciate that — can you show me the gap right now? I want to see it myself before I sign."*

A .028" gap causes rough running from the start and progressive damage over time. Do not accept the unit until gap is verified at .020" or corrected and documented.

---

## Water System — The Critical Walk (20 minutes)

> *"Is city water hooked up? I want to do a full pressurized walk before we go any further."*

Connect to city water, then walk every cabinet under every sink looking and listening for drips:

> *"I've read that some BackCountry Titanium units came off the line with water fill lines clamped with the wrong-size hardware that blows off on first connection. Let me get under pressure slowly and we'll see."*

After walking the trailer under pressure:

> *"Now I want to test the black tank flush valve. Can you run the tank flush for me?"*

Watch the flush: water should enter the tank in one direction through the flush port. If it seems to be flowing backward or the valve doesn't function correctly:

> *"This flush valve may be installed backwards — that's a documented issue on this model. Can you flag this for the service department to verify before I take the unit?"*

Then:

> *"Let's check every tank dump handle. I want to see that each one is physically connected to its valve — can you pull each one while I look at the valve?"*

---

## Stabilizer Jacks — The Honest Conversation (5 minutes)

Deploy the stabilizer jacks. Then rock the trailer side to side gently.

> *"The stabilizers on Outdoors RV trailers are a common complaint — the stock jacks aren't really adequate for a trailer at this price point. Can you show me what you have installed and confirm they're the standard setup?"*

You're not necessarily asking the dealer to fix it — you're confirming the situation so you can budget for replacement (plan on $300-500 before the first trip).

> *"I'll be replacing these before my first trip. Is there anything I should know about the installation on this specific chassis?"*

---

## Refrigerator Check (5 minutes)

> *"What temperature is the fridge reading right now? It's been on for a few hours — it should be below 40°F."*

If it's at or near ambient temperature after several hours on electric:

> *"I've read about a documented issue on Blackstone models where cutting oil in the gas line coats the burner assembly and reduces the flame, preventing proper cooling. Can your service department check the burner assembly before I take delivery?"*

**280RKS specific:**

> *"I understand the fridge is inaccessible when the slide is in during travel. Can you show me that? I want to see it with the slide retracted so I know what to expect on travel days."*

---

## Exterior, Roof, and Awning (20 minutes)

> *"I want to walk the roof. It's a walk-on design — can we get a ladder?"*

Walk the full roof. Inspect all sealant joints. Then walk the exterior perimeter:

> *"I'm going to photograph every exterior seal on all four sides before I drive away. I'll date-stamp everything as my maintenance baseline."*

**28BKS specific — awning seal:**

> *"I've read about rain leaks at the awning track on the Timber Ridge line. Can we deploy the full awning and I'll inspect the seal track where it meets the wall?"*

Inspect the seal where the awning track meets the trailer wall. Press along the full length — any separation needs to be noted and sealed before delivery.

---

## Floor Plan-Specific Checks

**28BKS — bottom bunk:**

> *"I want to open the door under the bottom bunk. I've read that this opens to the trailer interior, not exterior storage — I just want to confirm that so there are no surprises."*

**28DBS — tank flush valve:**

> *"Let's run the tank flush one more time with me watching the valve end to confirm the direction."*

**280RKS — slide + fridge interaction:**

> *"Let's retract the kitchen slide. I want to see firsthand that the fridge is behind the slide when it's in."*

**26RPS — rear private suite:**

> *"Let's run both slides out. I want to walk through the rear suite, verify the door closes for privacy, and test the bedroom TV."*

---

## Appliances (15 minutes)

> *"Let's work through all the appliances — A/C units, cooktop, range hood, furnace. I want to see everything run."*

- Both A/C units: run 5 minutes each, feel for cold air at vents
- All cooktop burners: light and hold
- Range hood: all speeds, confirm ventilation direction
- Furnace: one full heat cycle, confirm heat at all registers

---

## Walkthrough Wrap-Up

> *"Here's my list of items for the PDI form."*

State everything you found. Then:

> *"For anything that can be fixed today, I'd like it fixed and retested before I sign. For anything that needs to be scheduled — generator spark plug gap, flush valve direction, auto leveling re-zero — I want it written as a 'fix before delivery' item with a specific date. I'll come back."*

**The "we'll handle it after" response:**

> *"I'd rather not take delivery until these are resolved. Can we set a specific return date? I'm happy to wait for a quality unit."*

---

## Final Questions Before Signing

> *"What's the typical turnaround for warranty parts from Outdoors RV manufacturing? I've read about long lead times — I want to know what to expect."*

> *"Is there a dedicated Outdoors RV technician on staff here? Not just an authorized dealer, but someone who knows the ORV chassis and suspension specifically?"*

> *"If I have a Ground Control leveling issue in the field, what's the fastest path to support — you, Lippert directly, or Outdoors RV?"*

The answers tell you exactly what your ownership experience will look like before you sign.

---

## Final Checklist Before Keys Change Hands

- [ ] Signed PDI form with all findings noted
- [ ] Generator spark plug gap verified at .020" (26RPS, 28DBS)
- [ ] Auto leveling system demonstrated and working
- [ ] Tank flush valve direction verified
- [ ] Water system pressure-walked with no blown clamps
- [ ] Fridge at temperature
- [ ] All "fix before delivery" items resolved OR scheduled in writing with date
- [ ] Dated photos of all exterior seals
- [ ] Generator hour meter reading logged
- [ ] Full warranty document in hand
- [ ] Service department direct contact info — name, phone, email
- [ ] Auto leveling re-zero procedure documented for field use
