# Outdoors RV Titanium Series - Deep Dive: All 4 Floor Plans
*BackCountry 26RPS · BackCountry 28DBS · Blackstone 280RKS · Timber Ridge 28BKS*
*Research date: 2026-05-22*

---

## The Brand Context

Outdoors RV Manufacturing is based in La Grande, Oregon — a smaller-batch producer that builds for mountain regions, four-season camping, and western backcountry. This is not an Indiana mega-factory product. The community consensus across [iRV2](https://www.irv2.com/forums/f282/), [IFish](https://www.ifish.net/threads/any-outdoor-rv-trailer-owners.1645345/), and [RV Insider](https://www.rvinsider.com/Outdoors-Rv-RV-Reviews?make=Outdoors+RV) is consistent: these trailers are built better than 75% of what's on the market, and real owners back that up with multi-year ownership accounts. The issues are real too — but they're different in character from Alliance's problems. ORV issues are mostly component selections (inadequate jacks, occasional wrong-size fitting) rather than structural workmanship failures.

**The Titanium Series** is the premium tier — same chassis, suspension, and four-season construction as the Mountain Series, but adds Lamilux HD 4000 high-gloss silver fiberglass exterior, Ground Control TT auto leveling, frameless thermal pane windows, wireless camera system, leather materials, and raised panel cabinets. Premium over Mountain Series: ~$7,000-$10,700 depending on floor plan and model year.

## Floor Plan 1: BackCountry Titanium 26RPS

### "Rear Private Suite" — The Couples Destination Trailer

**RPS = Rear Private Suite.** This is not a rear kitchen — it's a dedicated second bedroom at the rear of the trailer, making the 26RPS effectively a two-bedroom layout in a 32-foot package: front bedroom + rear private suite + center living/kitchen. Two slides add interior volume when set up.

### Specs

| Spec | Value |
|---|---|
| Length | 31'8" |
| Width | 8' |
| Height | 13'1" |
| Slides | 2 |
| Sleeps | 3 |
| Fresh water | 100 gal |
| Gray water | 65 gal |
| Black water | 40 gal |
| Generator | Onan 3.6k LP (standard) |
| Solar | 400W rooftop + off-grid battery rack (4 batteries) |
| MSRP (2026 est.) | ~$85K-$95K |
| Tow vehicle | 3/4-ton minimum |

### What Owners Say

The 26RPS has a smaller owner community than the 28DBS but draws consistent praise from destination campers and couples who want a genuine second sleeping area separate from the main bedroom. The rear suite makes it ideal for couples traveling with adult guests or adult children. Per [iRV2 Outdoors RV Owners Forum](https://www.irv2.com/forums/f282/), the two-slide configuration opens up the center living area significantly.

The 400W solar + 4-battery rack makes this the most off-grid-capable of the four floor plans out of the box. The 100-gal fresh water tank backs that up for multi-day stays.

### Known Issues

- Stabilizer jacks: stock units inadequate — same complaint as every ORV model. Budget for replacement before first trip
- Water fill line clamp sizing: verify at PDI (documented across BackCountry line)
- Generator: verify spark plug gap (.020" not .028") at delivery — documented factory error
- Auto leveling: Titanium models may need re-zeroing; have dealer demonstrate full cycle at delivery
- Height (13'1") requires attention to low clearances and adds wind drag

### Best For
Couples or small groups who want destination camping with genuine privacy, full off-grid capability, and don't need family sleeping capacity.

## Floor Plan 2: BackCountry Titanium 28DBS

### "Double-over-Double Bunkhouse" — The Full-Timer Family Hauler

The 28DBS is the most thoroughly documented floor plan in this group and the one with the richest owner community. [Living Tiny With A Wolf](https://livingtinywithawolf.com/outdoors-rv-review/) full-timed in a 28DBS for over a year and wrote the definitive long-form owner review. The short version: "exceptional quality at excellent value."

### Specs

| Spec | Value |
|---|---|
| Length | 33' |
| Slides | 1 |
| Sleeps | 9 |
| Fresh water | 100 gal + 10 gal hot water |
| Gray water | 40 gal + 40 gal galley gray |
| Black water | 40 gal |
| Dry weight | ~8,680 lbs |
| GVWR | ~11,000 lbs |
| Loaded real-world weight | ~10,000 lbs |
| Entry doors | 2 (dual entry) |
| Generator | Onan 3.6k LP (standard) |
| Solar | 400W rooftop |
| MSRP (2026) | ~$100,140 (sale ~$84,880) |
| Tow vehicle | 3/4-ton or 1-ton strongly recommended |

### What Owners Say

Per [Living Tiny With A Wolf](https://livingtinywithawolf.com/outdoors-rv-review/): ground clearance is exceptional — full-time boondocking across Southern Utah, Northern Montana, Southern Oregon, and Northern California with zero off-road issues. The dual 50-gallon gray tanks (40 gal regular + 40 gal galley gray) are uniquely generous and a real advantage for extended stays without hookups.

Per [iRV2 BackCountry 28DBS thread](https://www.irv2.com/threads/what-are-your-thoughts-on-a-backcountry-28dbs.1935371/): owners consistently note the trailer is tongue-heavy; a 3/4-ton or 1-ton is not optional, it's mandatory for safe towing. At 11.5 feet tall, fuel economy takes a hit and low clearances require attention.

The single slide (vs 2 on the 26RPS) means simpler mechanics — one slide to maintain, one seal to inspect, one mechanism to fail. For a family full-timer where reliability beats space maximization, that's a real advantage.

### Known Issues

- **Back flush valve installed backwards** — documented on this model. Test at PDI: run a tank flush and verify flow direction
- **Generator spark plug gap** — .020" spec, some units shipped with .028". Ask dealer to verify at delivery. One 2022 Backcountry owner discovered this at 20 hours run time; later fuel system failure
- **Stabilizer jacks** — replace before first trip, universally
- **Tongue weight** — plan for weight-distribution hitch and sway control. This trailer requires proper hitch setup
- **Height** — 11'5" is manageable but watch campsite clearances and bridges
- One reported issue: A/C ducts not cut out from roof unit at factory — 30-minute fix but annoying

### Best For
Families who camp hard, full-timers, and anyone needing serious sleeping capacity (9) with genuine four-season and off-road capability. The most battle-tested floor plan in this group.

## Floor Plan 3: Blackstone Titanium 280RKS

### "Rear Kitchen + Front Bedroom" — The Premium Couples Entertainer

The Blackstone is ORV's upscale brand, positioned above BackCountry in the lineup. The 280RKS is the rear kitchen configuration — cook faces the living area, counter wraps around the rear, pantry next to the fridge. The Blackstone has been in production for many years and has the most long-term ownership data of the four floor plans.

### Specs

| Spec | Value |
|---|---|
| Length | 34'11" |
| Width | 8' |
| Interior height | 6'11" |
| Slides | 2 |
| Sleeps | 5 |
| Dry weight | 8,690 lbs |
| GVWR | 11,000 lbs |
| Hitch weight | 805 lbs |
| Cargo capacity | 2,790 lbs |
| Fresh water | 80 gal |
| Gray water | 40 + 40 gal |
| Black water | 50 gal |
| Furnace | 35K BTU |
| MSRP (2026 est.) | ~$80K-$90K |
| Tow vehicle | 3/4-ton or 1-ton |

### What Owners Say

Per [iRV2 Black Stone 280RLSB or 280RKSB thread](https://www.irv2.com/threads/black-stone-280rlsb-or-280rksb.1693663/): owners who have run the 280RKSB for 2+ years report very few road disturbance issues — the ORV suspension absorbs washboard and rough roads well enough that items rarely move in the rear kitchen. This is the #1 concern with rear kitchens and ORV's suspension engineering addresses it effectively.

The rear kitchen specific layout plus: one owner specifically chose it because there's no overhead cabinet over the kitchen sink — at 6'4" tall, that's a meaningful clearance gain for taller owners.

**The fridge caveat:** when the slide is retracted for travel, the refrigerator is inaccessible. Owners keep a small cooler in the tow vehicle for road stops. This is a known design tradeoff for the 280RKS, not a defect — but know it going in.

Per [RV Insider Blackstone reviews](https://www.rvinsider.com/Outdoors-Rv-Black-Stone-RV-Reviews?manu=Outdoors+RV&brand=Black+Stone): one 3-year owner reports zero major problems and praises the build quality; one noted the Outdoors RV manufacturing plant visit experience was poor (indifferent/rude treatment), but the trailer itself was excellent.

### Known Issues

- **Stabilizer jacks** — budget for replacement (same as all ORV models)
- **Refrigerator gas line** — one documented case of cutting oil in the gas line coating the burner assembly and reducing the flame, causing poor cooling. Test: verify fridge reaches temperature at PDI (should be below 40°F after several hours on)
- **Wireless remote control** — the all-in-one remote for slides, awning, lights, and stabilizers. Verify all functions at PDI before leaving
- **Fridge inaccessible when slide retracted** — not a defect, but plan for it on travel days
- **Height/length** — at 34'11" this is the longest floor plan in the group; confirm your campsite can handle it

### Best For
Couples who cook seriously, entertain at camp, and want the most premium ORV experience. The Blackstone's long production history means more owner data, more forum knowledge, and better parts availability than newer models.

## Floor Plan 4: Timber Ridge Titanium 28BKS

### "Bunkhouse + Front Bedroom" — The Lighter Family Four-Season Trailer

The Timber Ridge is ORV's third product line — positioned between the BackCountry and Blackstone in style but focused on family camping. The 28BKS is the bunkhouse configuration with a front private bedroom, making it the closest ORV equivalent to the Alliance Delta 281BH — except heavier, more capable, and better built.

### Specs

| Spec | Value |
|---|---|
| Length | 32'8" |
| Dry weight | ~7,975 lbs |
| Slides | 1 |
| Sleeps | 9 |
| Entry doors | 2 (dual entry) |
| TV | Swing-out 40" |
| Bath | Skylight |
| A/C | 15K BTU |
| MSRP (2026 est.) | ~$70K-$80K |
| Tow vehicle | 3/4-ton minimum |

### What Owners Say

Per [iRV2 Outdoors RV Owner Since 2022: Seeking 28BKS Insights](https://www.irv2.com/forums/f282/outdoors-rv-owner-since-2022-seeking-28bks-insights-670430.html): owners describe it as the best travel trailer they've ever owned, with 140+ nights of heavy use looking like new. The off-road design holds up where other trailers show wear.

Per [iRV2 Timber Ridge Yay or Nay thread](https://www.irv2.com/threads/timber-ridge-yay-or-nay.1851625/): "better than 75% of the other stuff on the market" is the consistent framing — some warranty wait time issues, but the build itself holds up.

**The bottom bunk surprise:** the space under the bottom bunk has a door that opens to the trailer interior, not exterior storage. First-time owners often expect exterior gear storage access and are surprised. Know this before delivery.

The 28BKS has the lightest dry weight of the four floor plans (~7,975 lbs) and a single slide — giving it the simplest mechanical profile in this group. At 32'8", it fits better in smaller campsites than the 34'11" Blackstone.

### Known Issues

- **Stabilizer jacks** — replace before first trip (same as all ORV models)
- **Plumbing connections** — Timber Ridge line broadly has documented plumbing issues: table construction, rain leaks from awning, bench seats not staying in place, frame rust on older models
- **Awning rain leaks** — documented on Timber Ridge line. Test awning seal carefully at PDI and add to seal inspection checklist
- **Bottom bunk interior door** — not a defect but surprises owners; no exterior storage access under bottom bunk
- **Warranty wait times** — reported across all ORV models

### Best For
Families who want serious four-season and moderate off-road capability in the lightest, mechanically simplest package of these four options.

## Is the Titanium Upgrade Worth It?

Per [iRV2 Backcountry vs Titanium](https://www.irv2.com/forums/f282/backcountry-vs-titanium-519879.html) and [Cost/Difference Mountain vs Titanium](https://www.irv2.com/forums/f282/cost-difference-between-mountain-and-titanium-editions-353510.html):

**Worth it for:**
- Frameless windows — very difficult to add yourself; structural change
- Ground Control TT auto leveling — transforms campsite setup
- Lamilux fiberglass exterior — long-term durability win over aluminum skin; resale value

**Less compelling for:**
- Leather materials — wears similarly to Mountain Series vinyl in most conditions
- Camera system — aftermarket alternatives exist for less money

**Consensus:** If you're buying ORV at this price point, pay the Titanium premium. The frameless windows and auto leveling alone justify most of it. The fiberglass exterior justifies the rest for long-term ownership.
