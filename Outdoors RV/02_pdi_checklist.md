# Outdoors RV Titanium Series - PDI Checklist
*BackCountry 26RPS · BackCountry 28DBS · Blackstone 280RKS · Timber Ridge 28BKS*
*Built from documented owner issues on iRV2, RV Insider, Living Tiny With A Wolf, IFish, and PissedConsumer*
*Date: 2026-05-22*

Items marked **🔴 CRITICAL** are documented factory defects — verify on every unit.

---

## Before You Arrive

Call the dealer the day before:
- Request the unit be connected to shore power and city water when you arrive
- Ask that the refrigerator has been running for at least 4 hours
- Request the generator be started and warmed up

---

## Auto Leveling System (Titanium Models — All Four Floor Plans 🔴)

The Lippert Ground Control TT auto leveling is the Titanium's flagship feature — and its first documented headache.

- [ ] **🔴 Have dealer demonstrate a full auto-level cycle from unlevel position** — system must complete without errors
- [ ] Verify the battery display and solar display agree on battery state — documented error: system reports "batteries low" while solar shows full; needs re-zeroing at the control head
- [ ] Ask dealer to walk you through the re-zero procedure and document it — you will likely need this in the field
- [ ] Confirm all four leveling jacks extend and retract fully under the auto-level command
- [ ] Verify the wireless remote controls slides, awning, exterior lights, and stabilizers (280RKS standard; confirm on other models)

---

## Generator (26RPS & 28DBS — Onan 3.6k Standard) 🔴

- [ ] **🔴 Start the generator and let it run for 10 minutes** — verify smooth, steady operation with no rough running or misfires
- [ ] **🔴 Ask dealer to pull one spark plug and show you the gap** — factory spec is .020"; documented units shipped with .028" (wrong gap causes rough running, later engine damage)
- [ ] Load the generator — turn on A/C while generator is running and verify it handles the load without sagging or shutting down
- [ ] Check generator exhaust for unusual smoke or smell
- [ ] Note the generator hour meter reading at delivery — log it

*280RKS and 28BKS: if generator is optional/not installed, skip this section.*

---

## Water System 🔴

- [ ] **🔴 Connect city water slowly and walk the full trailer under pressure** — documented factory defect across BackCountry and Titanium line: water fill lines attached with wrong-size clamps that blow off on first connection
- [ ] Run the system at full city water pressure for 30 minutes — walk every cabinet under every sink, behind toilet, under shower
- [ ] **🔴 Test the black tank flush valve direction** — documented on 28DBS and adjacent models: back flush valve installed backwards at factory. Run a tank flush and verify water enters the tank in the correct direction (in through the flush port, not reverse)
- [ ] Pull each tank dump handle and verify the actual valve moves — documented "not hooked up" handles across ORV line
- [ ] Turn on every individual fixture shutoff and verify each one cuts off its fixture
- [ ] Run the shower for 3 minutes — check bathroom floor outside pan for any leaking at the door seal
- [ ] Test the outdoor shower if equipped
- [ ] Verify all propane hose connections are secure and correctly sized — wrong-size fittings documented on ORV models; check regulator connections and appliance connections

---

## Refrigerator

- [ ] Verify refrigerator is at or below 40°F after running 4+ hours — documented gas-line issue on Blackstone: cutting oil coating the burner assembly reduces flame and prevents proper cooling
- [ ] **280RKS specific:** with both slides in, confirm you understand the fridge is inaccessible during travel — this is expected design, not a defect, but verify it and plan accordingly
- [ ] Test refrigerator on both electric and LP modes if absorption fridge equipped

---

## Stabilizer Jacks 🔴

- [ ] **🔴 Deploy all stabilizer jacks and test for stability** — stock ORV stabilizer jacks are universally criticized across all models and all price points. A $70K-$100K+ trailer with inadequate stabilizers is a documented pattern
- [ ] Rock the trailer side to side with jacks down — if there is meaningful flex, the jacks are undersized (expected finding; plan for immediate replacement)
- [ ] Note: budget $300-500 for aftermarket stabilizer upgrade (Lippert Trailaire, Equal-i-zer, or X-Chock wheel chocks) before first trip

---

## Slides (26RPS: 2 slides | 28DBS: 1 slide | 280RKS: 2 slides | 28BKS: 1 slide)

- [ ] Run all slides through 3 full in/out cycles — listen for straining, one-sided walking, overload stop engagement
- [ ] Walk slide floor edges after extending — check for lifting or gaps at the transition
- [ ] Inspect slide rubber wiper seals — look for any factory damage or improper seating
- [ ] Verify slide membrane (top seal on slide) is intact
- [ ] Check the hardwood slide fascia for any damage or loose attachment

---

## Exterior & Roof Seals

- [ ] **Walk the full roof** — inspect every lap sealant joint at both A/C bases, all vent corners, antenna, and any penetrations
- [ ] Inspect all exterior seams at corners and sidewall panel joints
- [ ] **Timber Ridge 28BKS specific:** test the awning seal carefully — rain leaks documented at awning-to-trailer junction on Timber Ridge line. Deploy the awning and inspect the seal track where it meets the wall
- [ ] **Photograph every exterior seal on all four sides before leaving the lot** — dated photos are your documentation baseline
- [ ] Check the Lamilux fiberglass front cap for any cracks or separation at the seam where it meets the sidewall — verify fully bonded

---

## Interior - Floor Plans and Quirks

**28BKS specific:**
- [ ] **Open the door under the bottom bunk** — this door opens to the trailer interior, NOT exterior storage. Confirm you understand this before delivery — first-time owners are commonly surprised
- [ ] Test dual entry doors — both should open, close, and latch cleanly
- [ ] Test swing-out 40" TV — verify it pivots and locks in viewing position

**280RKS specific:**
- [ ] Test rear kitchen: all burners light, range hood vents, pantry pull-out slides cleanly
- [ ] Test dual lounge recliners — verify recline function and return
- [ ] Test skylight over dinette — opens and seals closed

**26RPS specific:**
- [ ] Test rear private suite — verify door closes and provides privacy, bedroom lighting works, 12V TV functions
- [ ] Test both slide configurations (two slides) — confirm living area opens properly with both extended

**28DBS specific:**
- [ ] Test all bunk ladders for stability — apply lateral load to each
- [ ] Verify each bunk's individual light and outlet (if equipped)
- [ ] Test both entry/exit doors — open, close, latch from inside and outside

---

## Appliances (All Models)

- [ ] Both A/C units to cold — run 5+ minutes, verify compressor engages and cold air comes from vents
- [ ] Light all cooktop burners — verify ignitors and flame hold
- [ ] Run range hood on all speeds — confirm venting
- [ ] Run furnace for one full heat cycle — confirm ignition sequence, blower, and heat at all registers
- [ ] Test all electrical outlets with outlet tester

---

## Chassis, Tow, and Tires

- [ ] Verify lug nut torque (ask dealer to confirm with torque wrench)
- [ ] Inspect tire date codes (DOT last 4 digits) — should be within 2 years of manufacture
- [ ] Check tire sidewalls for cracking
- [ ] Test all running lights, brake lights, turn signals via tow vehicle
- [ ] **Verify your tow vehicle payload against actual trailer weight** — all four floor plans require 3/4-ton minimum; the 28DBS loads to ~10,000 lbs and requires proper hitch setup with weight distribution and sway control

---

## Documentation — Before Signing

- [ ] Signed PDI form with all findings noted
- [ ] Any "fix before delivery" items resolved or formally scheduled in writing
- [ ] Dated photos of every exterior seal — all four sides plus roof
- [ ] Full warranty document in hand
- [ ] Service department direct contact (not sales)
- [ ] Generator hour meter reading logged
- [ ] Auto leveling re-zero procedure documented

---

## Print-and-Bring: 10 Must-Test Items

1. Auto level full cycle (Titanium) — complete without error
2. Generator — 10-minute run, spark plug gap verified at .020"
3. City water connection slow — walk for blown clamps
4. Black tank flush — verify valve direction
5. Stabilizer jacks — assess stability (plan for replacement)
6. Fridge at temperature — below 40°F after 4+ hours
7. All slides — 3 cycles, check floor edges and wiper seals
8. Both A/C units to cold
9. Awning seal (28BKS especially) — inspect track and wall seal
10. Photograph every exterior seal before moving the unit
