# Outdoors RV Titanium Series - Seasonal Maintenance Schedule
*BackCountry 26RPS · BackCountry 28DBS · Blackstone 280RKS · Timber Ridge 28BKS*
*Date: 2026-05-22*

---

## What Makes ORV Maintenance Different from Other Brands

Outdoors RV uses a heavier, more robust construction than most competitors — but that construction introduces specific maintenance considerations:

- **Lamilux HD 4000 fiberglass exterior** requires different care than aluminum-skin trailers. No oxidation problem, but UV chalking can occur without protection. Different cleaning products apply.
- **Onan LP generator** (26RPS, 28DBS standard) requires its own scheduled maintenance independent of the trailer.
- **MorRyde AllTrek CRE4000 suspension** has greaseable wet bolts that need periodic attention — this is maintenance most RV owners don't know to do.
- **Ground Control TT auto leveling** has an annual service procedure.
- **Heavy trailer weight** (8,000-8,700 lbs dry) puts more stress on tires and wheel bearings than lighter trailers.

---

## Before Every Trip (15 minutes)

- [ ] Walk all four sides — inspect fiberglass exterior and all seam sealants for any new cracks or separations
- [ ] Check all compartment latches close and lock
- [ ] Verify propane tank enclosure is secured
- [ ] Run all slide(s) out and back in once before leaving
- [ ] Safety chain, breakaway cable, all lights confirmed
- [ ] Generator: if running on this trip, do a 2-minute pre-run to confirm clean operation

---

## After Every Trip (15 minutes)

- [ ] Walk exterior — note any new damage, seal changes, or road debris impact
- [ ] Clean debris from slide rubber wiper seals before it sits
- [ ] Log generator hours if used
- [ ] Note anything that rattled or malfunctioned while fresh

## Spring / Season Open (4-5 hours)
*Most important maintenance event of the year*

### Fiberglass Exterior — Annual Wash and Protect

The Lamilux HD 4000 fiberglass is more maintenance-friendly than aluminum skin but needs UV protection annually.

- [ ] Wash the full exterior with RV-safe cleaner (no petroleum solvents on fiberglass)
- [ ] Inspect the full surface for stress cracks, spider cracks, or chips — especially around slide corners and at the front cap seam
- [ ] Apply fiberglass oxidation restorer if any chalking is visible (Gel-Gloss or similar fiberglass-specific product)
- [ ] Apply UV protectant or wax to the full fiberglass exterior — one application per year minimum

### Roof & Seals — Annual Inspection and Documentation

- [ ] **Get on the roof and walk it fully** — inspect every sealant joint: A/C bases, vent corners, antenna, solar panel brackets, all penetrations
- [ ] **Photograph every sealant joint, dated** — your warranty and resale baseline
- [ ] Press-test every joint with fingernail — cracking or separation needs re-sealing immediately
- [ ] Re-seal any compromised joints with Dicor 501LSW (horizontal) or 505LSW (vertical) — clean area first, overlap old sealant by 1" each side, cure 24 hours before rain
- [ ] Inspect and re-seal all sidewall seams and corner caps
- [ ] **Timber Ridge 28BKS specific:** inspect the awning track seal — rain leaks documented at this location. Re-apply sealant along the full awning track where it meets the wall
- [ ] Log all seal work with product names, specific locations, and date

### Slide Seals

- [ ] Extend all slide(s) and inspect rubber wiper seals for cracks, tears, or separation
- [ ] Apply 303 Aerospace Protectant or Protect All to all rubber slide seals — prevents UV cracking
- [ ] Inspect slide membranes (top seals) for integrity
- [ ] Clean slide rails with dry cloth, apply silicone-based dry lubricant
- [ ] Retract slides and verify clean operation post-lube

### Generator Service — Annual (26RPS & 28DBS)

The Onan 3.6k LP generator has its own annual service schedule independent of trailer maintenance.

- [ ] **Change the spark plug(s) annually** — verify gap at .020" on installation (the documented factory-wrong-gap issue can recur if wrong plugs are used)
- [ ] Clean or replace the air filter
- [ ] Check and top off oil level
- [ ] Run under load for 20-30 minutes — vary the load. Generator exercising prevents stale fuel issues and keeps the carburetor clean
- [ ] Log hours run since last service
- [ ] Check exhaust system for any corrosion or loose connections

### MorRyde Suspension — Greaseable Wet Bolts

This is the maintenance item most ORV owners miss. The MorRyde AllTrek CRE4000 uses wet bolts (greaseable) that need periodic lubrication to maintain performance and prevent corrosion.

- [ ] Locate the grease fittings on the MorRyde suspension equalizers — there are multiple per axle
- [ ] Grease all fittings with a grease gun loaded with marine-grade grease or white lithium grease
- [ ] Inspect the rubber isolators in the suspension for cracking or deterioration
- [ ] Check shackle bolts for any loosening — torque per spec if any movement is found
- [ ] Inspect KYB shock absorbers for any leakage (oil seeping from the shock body = time to replace)

### Ground Control Auto Leveling — Annual Calibration

- [ ] Run a full auto-level cycle and verify no errors
- [ ] If any "batteries low" errors occur with full batteries, re-zero the system per the Lippert Ground Control TT manual
- [ ] Check all four leveling jack legs for any corrosion or binding in the retracted position
- [ ] Lubricate leveling jack screws with dry lubricant (not grease — it attracts dirt)

### De-Winterization

- [ ] Flush antifreeze from all lines — open every faucet until water runs clear
- [ ] Reconnect and inspect water filter
- [ ] Re-pressurize system — 30-minute pressure walk of entire trailer
- [ ] Test water pump on fresh tank
- [ ] Re-install any winterization plugs or bypasses
- [ ] **Verify water heater bypass is returned to normal before running hot water**

### Appliances — Annual Start-Up

- [ ] Both A/C units: run 20 minutes, verify cold air at all vents
- [ ] Furnace: fire one full heat cycle
- [ ] Water heater: full run cycle, verify temperature
- [ ] Refrigerator: 24 hours on electric, verify holds below 40°F
- [ ] Cooktop: light all burners — check for spider nests in orifices after winter storage
- [ ] LP system: inspect regulator and all hoses for cracking after winter

### Chassis & Tires

- [ ] Tire pressure (cold) before first trip — verify against spec for loaded weight
- [ ] Inspect sidewalls for cracking and date codes — replace tires over 5-6 years
- [ ] Check lug nut torque (110-120 ft-lbs typically — verify your spec)
- [ ] Inspect wheel bearing condition — grease packed bearings need annual repacking; sealed bearings need inspection for heat discoloration
- [ ] Inspect brakes through the wheel opening for pad wear

---

## Mid-Summer Check (1-2 hours)

- [ ] Roof spot check — focus on any seals that were borderline at spring open; UV summer heat accelerates sealant degradation
- [ ] **Fiberglass exterior mid-season inspection** — check for any new stress cracks, particularly around slide corners after the season's thermal cycling
- [ ] Re-condition slide rubber seals — summer heat dries them; second 303 application warranted
- [ ] Extend all slides and re-inspect membrane seals
- [ ] Run generator under load for 20 minutes if not used frequently (exercise cycle)
- [ ] Check MorRyde suspension grease fittings — re-grease if trailer has had significant off-road use since spring

---

## Fall / Season Close (4-5 hours)

### Final Seal Inspection Before Winter

- [ ] Full roof inspection — photograph and log everything, same protocol as spring
- [ ] Re-seal any joints showing degradation
- [ ] **28BKS awning track seal** — re-inspect and re-apply before winter
- [ ] **Log your fall seal work: date, locations, product names**

### Generator Winterization (26RPS & 28DBS)

- [ ] If storing in freezing temperatures: run the generator until fuel consumption is low, then add fuel stabilizer to the tank and run for 10 minutes to treat the entire fuel system
- [ ] Alternatively: run generator dry (run until it stops from fuel starvation) for long-term storage — LP generators tolerate this method
- [ ] Change oil if at annual interval

### Trailer Winterization

- [ ] **Option 1 (air blow-out):** compressor at max 50 PSI at city water inlet; blow each faucet from low points up; drain all three tanks first
- [ ] **Option 2 (antifreeze):** non-toxic pink RV antifreeze only; bypass water heater; run through system until pink appears at every faucet; pour in toilet and all drains for P-traps
- [ ] Remove water filter cartridge and store inside
- [ ] Leave low-point drain valves open during storage
- [ ] Retract all slides — apply 303 to rubber wiper seals before retracting, clean and dry-lube rails

### Battery and Solar Winterization

- [ ] Connect battery maintainer (smart charger, not trickle) during storage — heavy battery bank especially needs maintenance charge
- [ ] If disconnecting batteries: store in a temperature-regulated space if possible
- [ ] Verify solar panels are clean — bird droppings and debris reduce winter charging efficiency; brush off if needed
- [ ] 26RPS with 4-battery rack: verify all four batteries are in good health before winter — a weak cell in one battery can drag the whole bank

### Exterior and Tires

- [ ] Apply UV protectant to all rubber moldings, entry door seals, and fiberglass front cap edges
- [ ] Cover tires with UV covers if stored outdoors
- [ ] Move trailer once monthly if possible during storage to prevent flat spots
- [ ] Cover A/C shrouds — prevents debris and animal nesting

## Annual Service Items (Every 12 Months Regardless of Use)

- [ ] Generator: spark plug, air filter, oil (see above)
- [ ] Wheel bearing inspection — repack if packed bearings; inspect sealed units for heat
- [ ] MorRyde suspension greasing
- [ ] Brake inspection
- [ ] Ground Control TT leveling system calibration check
- [ ] Roof vent crank mechanism inspection and lubrication
- [ ] A/C coil cleaning (coil cleaner spray — not pressure water)
- [ ] Re-torque lug nuts after first 50 miles of season and again at 12 months
- [ ] CO and propane detector replacement if past rated service life (5-7 years)
- [ ] Smoke detector battery replacement
- [ ] Fire extinguisher charge check

## The Maintenance Log

Same structure as Alliance maintenance log — one entry per event with:
1. Date
2. What you inspected
3. What you found (including "nothing to report")
4. What you did (product names, specific locations)
5. Generator hours logged
6. Photos attached

ORV resale value holds better than most brands. A documented maintenance log with dated seal photos, generator service records, and suspension grease intervals is a meaningful differentiator at sale time.
