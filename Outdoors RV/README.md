### What the Titanium Package Adds (~$7,000-$10,700 over Mountain Series)

- **Lamilux HD 4000** high-gloss silver fiberglass exterior (vs. aluminum skin on Mountain)
- **Ground Control TT** auto leveling system — most praised Titanium feature
- **Frameless thermal pane windows** — hardest upgrade to DIY, highly recommended
- **Wireless rear and side camera system**
- **Leather interior materials** + raised panel cabinets
- **Silver fiberglass front cap**

Owner consensus on the [iRV2 Backcountry vs Titanium thread](https://www.irv2.com/forums/f282/backcountry-vs-titanium-519879.html): frameless windows and auto leveling alone justify most of the premium. Fiberglass exterior is a long-term durability win over aluminum.

## 5 Issues to Verify at PDI (All Models)

These are documented across ORV models — not theoretical risks:

1. **🔴 Stabilizer jacks** — universally criticized across all ORV models. Stock jacks are inadequate for a trailer in the $70K-$100K range. Budget for Lippert Trailaire or Equal-i-zer upgrade immediately.

2. **🔴 Water fill line clamps wrong size** — documented on multiple BackCountry and BackCountry Titanium models. Lines blow off on first water connection. Test: connect city water slowly and walk the fresh water system under pressure.

3. **🔴 Back flush valve installed backwards** — documented on 28DBS and adjacent models. Test: run a black tank flush and verify water flows in the correct direction.

4. **🔴 Generator issues (28DBS, 26RPS)** — documented incorrect spark plug gap from factory (.028" instead of specified .020") causing rough running. Ask dealer to show you the generator runs clean at delivery and pull a plug to verify gap.

5. **🔴 Auto leveling system errors (Titanium models)** — the Lippert Ground Control TT can report "batteries low" error while solar shows full. Needs re-zeroing. Have the dealer demonstrate a full auto-level cycle at delivery.

## The Titanium-Specific Buy Sequence

If you're seriously shopping any of these four:

1. **26RPS or 280RKS** → couples/destination focus. Check: 26RPS has Onan generator standard; 280RKS may not. Confirm what's included on your specific unit.
2. **28DBS** → families / full-timers. Heaviest tow in this group. Verify your truck's actual payload against a loaded 28DBS (~10,000 lbs) before committing.
3. **28BKS** → families wanting lighter bunkhouse. 1 slide = mechanical simplicity advantage. Note: bottom bunk has an interior-access door, not exterior storage — surprises owners.
4. **All four** → replace the stabilizer jacks before your first trip. Budget $300-500. This is not optional.
